# Ideal Body Weight Calculator — Semantic Demand Research

## Date: 2026-03-20

## People Also Ask (Google) [TRACED]
- "What should I weigh for my height?" — #1 question. Validation mode.
- "How much should I weigh at 5'4?" — Specific, height-anchored queries.
- "Is BMI or ideal body weight more accurate?" — Comparison mode.
- "What is ideal body weight?" — Definition seekers.
- "How close should I be to my ideal body weight?" — Range/flexibility question.

## User Emotional State [TRACED from search patterns]
- **Primary emotion:** Validation-seeking. Users want to hear "you're okay" or get a target to work toward.
- **Mental model:** "There's ONE number I should weigh" — but reality is a range. The calculator needs to gently correct this.
- **Sensitivity:** Weight is deeply personal. Clinical language ("obese," "underweight") can feel like judgment. Use warm framing.

## The ONE RULE [ASSUMED — synthesized from formula analysis]
"Your ideal weight is a range, not a number. Four formulas agree within 10-15 pounds."

This reframes the expectation from "tell me my number" to "here's your range." Reduces anxiety.

## User Language Patterns [TRACED]
- "What should I weigh?" (not "calculate my ideal body weight")
- "Am I a healthy weight?" (validation)
- "My doctor said I should lose weight" (external pressure)
- "These calculators never account for muscle" (frustration with limitations)

## Accent Color Rationale
- Warm sage green (#6dbc8a) — calming, health-associated, non-judgmental. Avoids red/warning associations.

## Falsifiability
- Devine/Robinson/Miller/Hamwi formulas are well-documented [TRACED]. All developed for medication dosing, not health targets.
- Frame size adjustment (10%) is a rough estimate [ASSUMED] — no precise clinical standard exists.
- "Range not a number" framing is editorially chosen [ASSUMED] — some users may want a single number.
