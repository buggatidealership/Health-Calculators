## User Mental Model
- Users think in TIMELINES: "If I do X for Y weeks, how much will I lose?"
- They want SPECIFIC NUMBERS, not ranges
- Primary emotional state: hopeful but anxious — "will this actually work for ME?"
- Weight loss vs fat loss confusion is universal — users equate scale weight with fat
- Water weight loss creates false early wins — users don't understand this
- Most users start with 16:8 because it's "just skipping breakfast"

## Exact User Language (from forums, Reddit, competitor sites)

## Emotional States
- **Before calculating:** Hopeful, slightly nervous, wanting validation
- **After seeing results:** Either relief ("that's doable") or disappointment ("only X lbs?")
- **Key fear:** "Will I lose muscle?" and "Is this safe?"
- **Key desire:** Seeing a timeline — week 1, week 2, month 1

## Competitor Gaps
- Most calculators show single-number output (boring, no context)
- None explain water weight vs fat loss clearly
- No timeline visualization (week-by-week projection)
- No "what happens to your body" context during fasting
- No emotional coaching ("this is normal, stick with it")

## SERP Classification
- **Query volume:** ~18K/mo for "fasting weight loss calculator"
- **SERP type:** MODERATE — established competitors but weak UX
- **Opportunity:** V25 dark design with timeline visualization + coach card differentiates strongly
