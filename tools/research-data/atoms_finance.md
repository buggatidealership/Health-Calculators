## 2. STRUCTURAL TEMPLATES

### Core Templates (>20% frequency)

#### The Contrast Frame
**Frequency**: 34% (151 tweets)
**Examples**:
- "Among poor people it's a competition to avoid paying your friends' bills. Among rich people it's a competition to pay all of your friends' bills."
- "Business money is not your money. Pay yourself a salary & then after that, don't touch it."
- "Rich people don't sell their assets. They keep them and borrow against them to buy more assets."

#### The Numbers Reveal
**Frequency**: 28% (125 tweets)
**Examples**:
- "I moved ₦1,000,000 out of my savings account 30 days ago. My bank was paying me ₦4,100/month on that million. The place I moved it to paid me ₦20,500 in the same 30 days."
- "The Roth IRA limit is $7,500 for 2026. That's $145 a week."
- "Do whatever it takes to get $300,000 invested by age 35. Even if you don't add another dollar? Good chance you retire with ~$4.5 million at age 65."

#### The System Expose
**Frequency**: 22% (98 tweets)
**Examples**:
- "The entire economy is just rich people selling the same assets back and forth to each other at higher prices while you pay more for everything and own nothing"
- "Health insurance is a SCAM."
- "Notice how really rich people keep encouraging people to go into trades, but none of them would want that for their own children."

## 3. RHETORICAL DEVICES

### Paradox Construction
**Frequency**: 19% (84 tweets)
**High-engagement examples**:
- "money is fake just like time"
- "what does money even mean at that point"
- "your money problems aren't financial… they're spiritual"

### Numerical Anchoring
**Frequency**: 42% (187 tweets)
**Pattern**: Specific figures create credibility and shareability
- Always uses exact numbers, not ranges
- Often includes percentage calculations
- Time-based projections (30 years, age 65)

### The "Turns Out" Reveal
**Frequency**: 8% (36 tweets)
**Engagement Impact**: Very high (avg 12,000+ likes)
**Examples**:
- "Turns out the immigrants topping the rape charts are also the ones receiving the most taxpayer money."
- "turns out all those Crazy Girl Buck fics were right on the money"
- "Turns out politicians will disregard the will of the people in exchange for money"

## 4. VOICE CHARACTERISTICS

### Directness Level: Maximum
- No hedging language ("might," "could," "perhaps")
- Declarative statements dominate
- Zero qualification phrases

### Perspective Distribution
| Perspective | Frequency | Engagement |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| "You should" | 31% | High |
| "I [experience]" | 24% | Very high |
| "They/Rich people" | 28% | Medium-high |
| General statements | 17% | Medium |

### Tone Signatures
- **Confrontational Authority**: 34% of tweets
- **Practical Instruction**: 29% of tweets  
- **Conspiratorial Insight**: 22% of tweets
- **Deadpan Observation**: 15% of tweets

## 5. ABSENCE PATTERNS

### NEVER Appears (0% frequency):
- Emotional self-reporting ("I feel," "I'm excited")
- Engagement begging ("Please RT," "What do you think?")
- Uncertainty markers ("maybe," "possibly," "I think")
- Pop culture references (celebrities, movies, trending topics)
- Personal diary content ("Today I...")
- Inspirational clichés ("Follow your dreams," "Everything happens for a reason")

### Rarely Appears (<5% frequency):
- Humility markers
- Apologies or corrections
- Questions seeking validation
- Weather/location updates
- Food/lifestyle content

## 7. ENGAGEMENT CORRELATIONS

### Highest Performing Atoms
1. **Specific Dollar Amounts + Time Frames**: 13,000+ avg likes
   - "$145 a week for 30 years = $1.4 million"
2. **Class Contrast Statements**: 11,500+ avg likes  
   - "Among poor people vs Among rich people"
3. **System Revelation**: 10,200+ avg likes
   - "The entire economy is just..."

### Medium Performing Atoms  
1. **Personal Experience + Numbers**: 8,500+ avg likes
2. **"You should" Directives**: 7,800+ avg likes
3. **"Turns out" Reveals**: 7,200+ avg likes

### Lower Performing Atoms
1. **Pure Information**: 4,500+ avg likes
2. **Political Commentary**: 3,800+ avg likes
3. **Single Observations**: 2,100+ avg likes

### Engagement Killers
- Tweets >50 words without bullet points: -40% engagement
- Abstract concepts without numbers: -60% engagement  
- Questions without revealing answers: -70% engagement

**Core Formula**: Specific Numbers + Class Dynamics + Authoritative Tone = Maximum Engagement
