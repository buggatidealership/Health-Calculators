## One Rule (Mental Model)
**"Transfer date + (266 minus embryo age) = due date"**

Or simplified:
- Day 3 transfer → add 263 days
- Day 5 transfer → add 261 days
- Day 6 transfer → add 260 days

For egg retrieval: add 266 days (Day 3) or 264 days (Day 5)

The underlying principle: 280 days from LMP equivalent. IVF lets you calculate the LMP equivalent precisely because you know the exact fertilization/transfer timing.

## Formula Verification (cross-referenced ACOG + multiple sources)
Standard: 280 days from LMP equivalent

**From Egg Retrieval:**
- Day 3 embryo: retrieval + 266 days (= 280 - 14 for LMP adjustment)
- Day 5 embryo: retrieval + 264 days
- Day 6 embryo: retrieval + 263 days

**From Transfer Date (Fresh):**
- Day 3: transfer + 263 days (266 - 3)
- Day 5: transfer + 261 days (266 - 5)
- Day 6: transfer + 260 days (266 - 6)

**From Transfer Date (FET):**
- Same as fresh transfer formulas (frozen vs fresh doesn't change math)
- Day 3: transfer + 263 days
- Day 5: transfer + 261 days
- Day 6: transfer + 260 days

**Gestational Age from Transfer:**
- GA days = days since transfer + (14 + embryo age)
- This converts to LMP-equivalent dating

**Note:** Some sources show Day 5 FET = 259 days. The discrepancy is 261 vs 259. ACOG-aligned sources use 261 (266-5). The existing calculator uses 259. Going with 261 per ACOG standard formula (280 - 14 - 5 = 261). Cross-check: transfer + 261 days means 261/7 = 37.28 weeks from transfer, plus 19/7 = 2.71 weeks embryo+LMP adjustment = 40 weeks total. Confirmed correct.

## Falsifiability
- The 261 vs 259 discrepancy for Day 5 FET: if clinical practice uses 259, our formula may be off by 2 days. Impact: minimal (due dates are estimates anyway) but worth noting.
- Emotional state data is thin (71 tweets). The anxiety finding could be sampling bias from Twitter's tendency toward emotional expression.
- "People Also Ask" data synthesized from competitor pages, not directly from Google SERP (search didn't return raw PAA boxes).
