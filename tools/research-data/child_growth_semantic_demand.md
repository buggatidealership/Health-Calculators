## User Emotional State [TRACED from search patterns]
- **Primary emotion:** Parental anxiety mixed with hope. "Is my kid okay?" is the unstated question.
- **Mental model:** "Higher percentile = better" — but this is wrong. The trend over time matters more than any single measurement.
- **Comparison drive:** Parents compare to siblings, peers, and their own childhood. This is natural but often misleading.

## The ONE RULE [ASSUMED — synthesized from pediatric guidance]
"The trend matters more than the number. A consistent 25th percentile is perfectly healthy."

This directly addresses the #1 misconception (higher = better) and reduces unnecessary anxiety.

## User Language Patterns [TRACED]
