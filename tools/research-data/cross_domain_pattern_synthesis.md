## The 10 Generating Principles

| # | Problem | Generating Principle |
|---|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | Entry/Arrival | Any system under continuity constraints must amortize the information cost of novelty across time. Every real arrival is a curve, not a step. |
| 2 | Response/Feedback | A response is evidence that input was received, absorbed, transformed, and returned — at timing that encodes processing depth, with intensity proportional to input investment. |
| 3 | Revelation | Progressive disclosure is not information rationing. It is the sequential construction of the interpretive context required for information to be meaningful. |
| 4 | State Change | A transition is not a gap between two states. It is a third state whose function is to transform the traveler, not just transport them. |
| 5 | Rhythm/Pacing | Rhythm is the management of prediction. Entrain, then violate, then resolve — at multiple timescales simultaneously. The optimal distribution is 1/f (pink noise). |
| 6 | Attention | Don't guide the eye. Tilt the floor. Attention is directed by shaping the field so that the target is the point of lowest potential energy. |
| 7 | Trust | Trust is the integral of consistent, costly signals over varied contexts, with asymmetric response to positive and negative inputs. It phase-transitions, not accumulates. |
| 8 | Departure/Exit | An ending is a transfer, not a termination. A figure dissolves into its ground. |
| 9 | Grouping | Belonging is communicated through shared participation in a field, separated by threshold discontinuity, structured by hierarchical nesting. Grouping IS meaning. |
| 10 | Tension/Resolution | The value of the release is not intrinsic to the release. It is proportional to the loading that preceded it. Loading is not a cost — it is where value is created. |

## 49 Novel Design Rules (summary)

Full domain analyses and derivations are in the session context of Mar 21, 2026.

### Top 10 Most Actionable for HealthCalculators

1. **Nucleation** — first element contains structural geometry of everything that follows
2. **Substrate** — each interaction changes how user sees, not just what they know
3. **Back-channel signaling** — system says "uh-huh" during extended input
4. **Gradient surround** — de-emphasize surroundings, don't just brighten target
5. **Loading state as product** — show calculation dimensions resolving, not spinner
6. **Ritardando exit** — last 15% of exit takes 40%+ of total duration
7. **Shared field grouping** — same easing curve = same group (gradient IS the container)
8. **Three-suspension limit** — never more than 3 unresolved conceptual threads
9. **Pink noise pacing** — variation at all timescales simultaneously
10. **The threshold is its own state** — 300-600ms where system visibly works

### Three Cross-Problem Meta-Patterns

1. **Bandwidth Principle** — all real transitions must be distributed across time (instant = infinite derivative = overload)
2. **Loading Principle** — value is created during preparation, not during display
3. **Field Principle** — direction/grouping/attention achieved by shaping environment, not labeling target

## Time & Space Synthesis (10 additional sub-dimensions)

### TIME

| # | Sub-dimension | Generating Principle | Top Rules |
|---|--------------|---------------------|-----------|
| 11 | Duration | Duration encodes value-weight as a ratio relative to a temporal baseline. It should emerge from content structure, not be imposed decoratively. | Duration scale (100/250/500/1200/2500ms), result duration proportional to computational density, pause at transitions determines if destination feels earned |
| 12 | Sequence | Sequence is a chain of context transformations. Each element delivers content AND transforms the interpretive frame for everything after it. | Frame before content, contour rule (rising=anticipation, falling=resolution), priority effect (first elements foreclose alternatives), middle deserves promotion or elimination |
| 13 | Simultaneity | Simultaneity communicates STRUCTURE (relationship, comparison). Sequence communicates CAUSATION (narrative, transformation). | Commutativity test (if swap changes nothing, show simultaneously), niche differentiation (simultaneous elements must occupy different perceptual niches), binding principle (elements perceived as one unit must appear at the same time) |
| 14 | Memory/Anticipation | The present is a membrane between compressed past and anticipated future. Meaning is the delta between what was carried forward and what is expected ahead. | Compress don't replay (momentum not event logs), make future felt not shown (dominant-seventh pull), design entry to design arrival (compression before payoff), references point both ways (anaphora/cataphora) |
| 15 | Tempo/Rate | Systems respond to the derivative, not the state. 1st derivative captures attention. 2nd encodes emotion. 3rd triggers arousal. | Design acceleration curve not duration, deceleration carries more info than acceleration, the hold is the loudest moment (fermata), match rate to emotional arc not informational arc |

### SPACE

| # | Sub-dimension | Generating Principle | Top Rules |
|---|--------------|---------------------|-----------|
| 16 | Proximity | Distance encodes interaction strength via non-linear decay with categorical thresholds (not a gradient). | 4x threshold (intra-group ≤1/4 inter-group), three-zone system (intimate/relational/contextual), edge-effect (boundaries are highest-energy zones), metric-choice (visual/semantic/temporal distance can conflict) |
| 17 | Depth | Depth is a cognitive instruction, not a visual style. Each layer transforms conditions for layers below it. | Blur is reverb (2-4px/8-16px/20px+ = three distances), 4 depth planes max, background is active participant, right-branch don't center-embed, depth transitions need directional logic |
| 18 | Scale | Size is not magnitude — it is regime change. Crossing a size threshold changes which cognitive mode the viewer enters. | Three regimes (atmospheric >50% viewport / narrative 15-50% / instrumental <15%), ratio ladder with consistent intervals, proportion shift as narrative event, viewport-to-element ratio = authority-to-autonomy |
| 19 | Direction | Direction is the spatial encoding of effort (vertical), time (horizontal), and attention (depth). Never neutral. | Vertical: up=effort/aspiration, down=delivery/settling. Horizontal: with reading flow=progress, against=reversal. Depth: slow approach=invitation, fast=threat. Results settle DOWN. Errors move UP. |
| 20 | Enclosure | Boundaries are zones with their own properties. The most meaningful events happen AT the boundary. | Borders are zones not lines, control permeability not opacity, boundaries generate their own content (hover states ARE ecotones), boundaries don't recurse (no bordered cards in bordered cards), graduated enclosure = intimacy gradient |
