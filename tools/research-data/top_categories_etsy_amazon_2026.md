# Top Product Categories: Etsy and Amazon (2025-2026)

**Date compiled:** 2026-03-28
**Sources:** Etsy investor reports, Marmalead keyword data, linkmybooks.com category analysis, Jungle Scout seller reports, Thunderbit FBA statistics, BeautyMatter Q4 2025 data, Momentum Commerce category forecasts, AMZ Prep marketplace data
**Methodology:** Web searches targeting 2025-2026 data; direct fetch of source pages; cross-verification of category share claims across minimum 2 independent sources. Findings not corroborated by 2 sources are flagged.

---

## Platform Context

**Etsy (2024 actuals, Etsy marketplace only):**
- Gross Merchandise Sales: $10.9 billion (down 8.6% YoY — platform is declining, not growing)
- Revenue: $2.02 billion marketplace revenue
- Active buyers: 89.6 million
- Active sellers: 5.6 million (post-verification purge)
- Habitual buyers (6+ purchases, $200+/yr): 6.4 million — drive 41% of GMS
- Mobile share: 68% of GMS originates from mobile
Source: Etsy Q4 2024 Investor Press Release (investors.etsy.com)

**Amazon FBA (2025-2026):**
- 60% of Amazon's total sales come from third-party sellers
- ~2.5 million active sellers globally
- ~30,000 FBA sellers earned over $1 million in 2026
- Average FBA seller revenue: $160,000/yr (median: $35,000 — wide spread, median is the operative number)
- Independent sellers averaged $290,000 in annual sales per Threecolts 2025 data
Source: Thunderbit FBA Statistics 2026, Threecolts 2025 category analysis

## ETSY — Top 3 Categories

### Methodology note
Category GMS share figures come from linkmybooks.com (citing Etsy internal data) and thunderbit.com (aggregating Etsy investor disclosures). The exact percentages differ slightly between sources; ranges are given where they conflict. Etsy does not publish category-level GMS as a separate line item in investor filings — these are third-party estimates from accounting/analytics vendors who work with Etsy seller data.

---

### Category 1: Home & Living (Homewares & Home Furnishings)

**GMS share:** 30–35% of Etsy GMS
**Estimated revenue:** ~$3.3–3.8 billion (applied to $10.9B marketplace GMS)
**Position:** Consistent #1 category by volume

**Top specific products within category:**
- Wall art (printable and physical) — boho line art, abstract minimalist prints
- Throw pillows and pillow covers
- Candles (especially soy, hand-poured, custom-labeled)
- Personalized doormats
- Mugs (teacher-themed, pet-parent niche, color-changing)
- Wreaths and seasonal decor
- Digital art prints (sold as instant downloads)

**Price ranges:**
- Digital wall art prints: $2–$8
- Physical wall art (framed/unframed): $15–$85
- Candles: $10–$45
- Custom mugs: $12–$30
- Throw pillows: $18–$65

**Buyer demographics:**
- Predominantly female (58–59% of all Etsy buyers are women)
- Core age bracket: 25–44 (66% of buyers are in this range)
- US-dominant: 56% of platform traffic
- Strong gifting behavior — habitual buyers skew toward home decor for gifting occasions

**What makes a listing stand out:**
1. Personalization option (custom name, date, family name) — approximately 1 in 3 Etsy transactions involves customization
2. Lifestyle photography over white-background product shots — buyers are buying a feeling, not a spec
3. Seasonal variants offered within the same listing (e.g., Christmas vs. everyday colorways)
4. Niche aesthetic targeting ("cottagecore," "dark academia," "maximalist eclectic") — Etsy search data shows "eclectic home decor" searches up 719% YoY in 2025 (Marmalead)
5. Bundle listings (e.g., 5-print digital bundle) outperform single items on conversion rate

**Falsifiability flag:** GMS share estimates from third-party vendors, not from Etsy's own disclosed category-level data. If Etsy published granular category breakdowns, the 30–35% figure could be revised. Two independent sources converge on this range, which increases confidence but does not confirm it.

### Category 2: Jewelry & Personal Accessories

**GMS share:** ~18–30% of Etsy GMS (wide range across sources — see flag below)
**Estimated revenue:** $2.0–3.3 billion
**Position:** #1 or #2 depending on source; consistently top-2

**Top specific products within category:**
- Name necklaces and initial necklaces (personalized)
- Birthstone rings and stackable rings
- Handwriting jewelry (custom engraved from handwriting sample)
- Minimalist gold/silver earrings
- Friendship bracelets
- Beaded jewelry (8+ million listings in this subcategory alone per Podbase 2025)
- Pet-name or child-name engraved pieces

**Price ranges:**
- Dainty name necklaces: $18–$65
- Birthstone rings: $25–$120
- Custom handwriting pieces: $45–$200
- Earrings (simple): $10–$40
- Beaded bracelets: $8–$35

**Buyer demographics:**
- Strong gifting use case: Mother's Day, Valentine's Day, birthdays dominate purchase triggers
- "Ring" and "necklace" are the #1 and #3 rising search terms on Etsy going into Q4 2025 (Marmalead November 2025 data — ring +13%, necklace +10%)
- Top seller CaitlynMinimalist: 3.5+ million sales, exclusively personalized minimalist jewelry
- Buyers skew female gift-givers shopping for themselves or others

**What makes a listing stand out:**
1. Personalization infrastructure — ability to accept custom text input at order
2. Gifting presentation: packaging photos, gift-bag mockups, "ready to gift" copy
3. High review count with review photos showing worn pieces (social proof of real sizing/color)
4. Multiple metal options (gold, silver, rose gold) within one listing — reduces shopper indecision
5. Turnaround time stated explicitly in listing (critical for gift-deadline buyers)
6. SEO: "name necklace," "personalized jewelry," "gifts for her" remain high-competition, high-volume terms

**Falsifiability flag:** The GMS share range (18–30%) reflects two different measurement approaches — one source uses "share of search queries," another estimates from seller revenue data. The lower bound (18%) likely represents jewelry alone; the higher bound (30%) may include accessories broadly. This ambiguity cannot be resolved without Etsy's internal category data.

### Category 3: Digital Downloads

**GMS share:** ~9.6% of Etsy search volume (Marmalead); revenue contribution disproportionately high due to near-zero COGS
**Estimated revenue:** $600M–$1.1B (wide range — no direct disclosure from Etsy; inferred from search share and average ticket)
**Position:** #3 by transaction volume, arguably higher by profit contribution to sellers

**Top specific products within category:**
- Printable planners (daily, weekly, budget planners)
- Printable wall art / SVG files
- Wedding templates (invitations, programs, seating charts)
- Resume and CV templates (Canva-compatible)
- Budget spreadsheets (Google Sheets / Excel)
- Social media templates
- Lightroom presets
- Sewing and embroidery patterns
- Educational worksheets and homeschool curriculum

**Price ranges:**
- Single printable: $1.99–$8
- Template bundles: $5–$25
- Lightroom preset packs: $8–$40
- Full wedding invitation suite: $15–$50
- Planner bundles: $5–$20

**Buyer demographics:**
- Broad age range — budget planners attract 25–45 demographic; wedding templates skew 25–35
- Strong repeat buyer behavior in templates (same buyer purchases multiple templates from same shop)
- US and UK dominate; wedding templates have strong UK/Australia demand

**What makes a listing stand out:**
1. Mockup quality — buyers can't hold a digital product; mockup showing it printed and displayed is the entire conversion surface
2. Compatibility clarity — listing must state "Canva compatible," "Instant download," "No software required" explicitly
3. Variation listings — offer multiple color/style variants within one listing to capture broader search terms
4. Review velocity matters more than in physical categories — buyers are anxious about whether digital files actually work
5. Immediate post-purchase experience — sellers who include a "how to edit" PDF in the download see lower refund rates

**Falsifiability flag:** The GMS estimate for digital downloads is the weakest number in this report. Etsy does not disclose it, and search share proxy is imprecise (a $3 digital download and a $65 necklace contribute equally to "search share" but very differently to GMS). The "near-zero COGS advantage" is an inference about seller economics, not a verified Etsy platform metric.

## AMAZON — Top 3 Categories for Third-Party/FBA Sellers

### Methodology note
Amazon does not publish category-level revenue breakdowns for third-party sellers as a separate disclosure. Figures below are derived from: Jungle Scout 2025 State of the Seller report, AMZ Prep best-seller data, BeautyMatter Q4 2025 category analysis, Momentum Commerce forecasts (Beauty), and Threecolts 2025 category guide. Where a claim is from a single source, it is flagged.

---

### Category 1: Beauty & Personal Care

**Category size:** ~$36 billion total Amazon US sales (2024, Momentum Commerce forecast)
**Subcategory breakdown:**
- Hair Care: ~$9.2B (up 23.8% YoY in 2024)
- Skin Care: ~$8.9B (up 43.8% YoY in 2024)
- Makeup, oral care, fragrance: remainder
**FBA seller advantage:** Category opened to ungated third-party sellers in 2017; now one of the most competitive categories for 3P sellers
**Average seller margins:** 42.3% average (AMZ Prep data — single source, treat as directional)

**Top specific products (Q4 2025 actual best-sellers — BeautyMatter):**

| Rank | Product | Price (Q4 2025) |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Hero Cosmetics Mighty Patch Original | $17.91 |
| 2 | eos Shea Better Body Lotion — Vanilla Cashmere | ~$10–14 |
| 3 | Mrs. Meyer's Liquid Hand Soap | ~$8–12 |
| 4 | Medicube Zero Pore Pads | ~$25–35 |
| 5 | Biodance Bio-Collagen Real Deep Mask | ~$20–30 |

**Category price trend:** Average price in top-25 Beauty: $12.44 in Q4 2025 (down from $14.84 in Q3). Market is shifting toward affordable everyday essentials, not premium products.

**K-beauty signal:** 70% of K-beauty's $2B US market now occurs online. 740,000+ TikTok videos in Q3 2025 featured K-beauty routine education — "social education before Amazon search" is the dominant discovery path in this category.

**Buyer behavior:**
- Subscribe & Save is a primary conversion lever — products achieving >15% S&S rates signal strong repeat purchase to Amazon's algorithm and receive ranking boosts
- High-intent buyers arrive from TikTok/Instagram after seeing ingredient education content; they arrive on Amazon already brand-aware, not browsing
- Gift-giving spike in Q4 boosts average cart size

**What makes a listing convert:**
1. Subscribe & Save activation — without it, you lose algorithm support in repeat-purchase categories
2. First image shows the specific claim: "clears pores in 2 uses," not a lifestyle shot of someone smiling
3. Before/after imagery where FTC-compliant — highest-converting creative format in skincare (single source: Market Defense executives quoted in BeautyMatter)
4. Safety and ingredient transparency signals (USDA organic badge, "dermatologist tested," cruelty-free icon) above the fold — Gen Z and Millennials filter by these
5. A+ Content lifts conversion 8% on average per Amazon's own published data
6. Review velocity: moderate competition subcategories need 50–100 reviews to rank; high-competition subcategories need 500+

**Falsifiability flag:** The $36B figure is a 2024 forecast from Momentum Commerce — actual 2025 figure not yet publicly disclosed. Margin data (42.3%) is from AMZ Prep's own calculation framework, not an independent audit. These should be treated as order-of-magnitude estimates.

### Category 2: Health & Household (Vitamins, Supplements, Household Essentials)

**Category characteristics:** Largest by SKU count; dominated by repeat-purchase commodities and supplements
**Top brand concentration:** Top 10 brands capture ~60% of category revenue (Actowiz Metrics 2025 analysis)
**Average price increase:** $12.50 (2020) to $15.20 (2025) across category — 21.6% over 5 years (Actowiz Metrics)
**FBA seller margin range:** Supplements 40–60% (AMZ Prep); category-wide 30–45%

**Top specific products (2025 actual data):**

| Product | Category | Monthly Units (est.) | Price Range |
|---------|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Magnesium Glycinate supplements | Vitamins | Top BSR in category | $15–$35 |
| Nature Made Vitamin D3 2000 IU | Vitamins | 97,578 units/month | $12–$22 |
| Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Whey | Protein | Consistent top-5 | $29–$75 |
| Premier Protein Shake (multipack) | Protein RTD | High velocity | $28–$45 |
| Orgain Organic Vegan Protein Powder | Protein | Growing YoY | $25–$55 |

**Subcategory growth signals (2025):**
- "Preventative health" framing outperforms therapeutic claims in search CTR
- "Gut-brain axis" products (prebiotics, probiotics) are the fastest-growing new subcategory
- Gummies format is growing at the expense of capsules — higher price-per-serving but higher conversion

**Buyer behavior:**
- Highest Subscribe & Save adoption rate of any Amazon category — vitamins are habit purchases
- Repeat purchase interval is predictable (30-day supply = 30-day reorder cycle), making LTV modeling straightforward
- Product ratings above 4.5 stars generate 2x sales conversions vs. below 4.5 (Actowiz Metrics)
- Prime Day drives significant bulk-purchase behavior — 40–65% of some SKUs' annual sales occur in Q4 and deal events

**What makes a listing convert:**
1. Subscription pricing visible on the main offer — even a 5% S&S discount changes the buyer's mental frame from "purchase" to "routine"
2. Third-party certification badges: NSF, Informed Sport, USP — these are table stakes in supplements, not differentiators
3. Supplement Facts panel as a listing image (mandatory for supplements, but placement in slot 2 or 3 drives conversions for ingredient-literate buyers)
4. Bundle SKUs (90-day supply, 2-pack) consistently rank above single-unit SKUs in supplements — lower per-unit price, higher cart value
5. Review count is the primary ranking signal in this category — a product with 5,000 reviews at 4.4 stars typically outranks a product with 500 reviews at 4.8 stars

**Falsifiability flag:** Monthly unit estimate for Nature Made Vitamin D3 (97,578) is from Accio.com's estimation model, not Amazon's published data. Amazon does not publish unit sales. These estimates are derived from BSR rank and historical correlation models — treat as directional, not precise.

### Category 3: Home & Kitchen

**Category characteristics:** Most searched category on Amazon; highest total SKU competition; strong for FBA sellers in sub-niches where incumbents are not dominant brands
**Margin range:** 30–45% (AMZ Prep); "handmade/artisanal home" subcategory reaches 45–65%
**Competition reality:** Home & Kitchen ranks as one of the most saturated Amazon categories overall — success requires subcategory targeting, not broad entry

**Top specific products driving volume (2025):**

| Product type | Representative SKUs | Price range |
|-------------|--------------------|----|
| Insulated tumblers | Stanley Quencher, Owala FreeSip | $25–$55 |
| Air fryers | Cosori, Ninja (dominant brands) | $49–$129 |
| Sous vide accessories | Third-party attachments | $20–$60 |
| Storage and organization | Clear stackable bins, drawer dividers | $12–$45 |
| Smart LED lighting | Color-changing strips, bulbs | $15–$35 |
| Renter-friendly shelves | Floating shelves, no-drill mounts | $18–$60 |

**FBA seller opportunity within category:**
- Accessories outperform flagship products — chargers, replacement parts, compatible accessories for dominant brand products (e.g., Stanley cup accessories, Instant Pot liners) have healthier margins and cheaper PPC than the flagship category (beBOLD Digital 2026 analysis)
- Micro-niches outperform broad plays: "best seller" in "under-sink organizers" is a more achievable target than "best seller in storage & organization"
- Seasonal products (holiday serveware, outdoor BBQ accessories) allow Q4 inventory spikes without year-round carrying costs

**Buyer behavior:**
- Kitchen purchase decisions are highly influenced by social media virality (Stanley cup example: $750M+ in annual revenue after TikTok virality)
- Home office accessories see sustained demand post-2020 — remote work is a permanent structural driver, not a trend
- Price sensitivity is higher than Beauty — buyers comparison-shop across 5–10 listings on average before converting
- "Deals" tab and lightning deal participation significantly boost conversion in this category

**What makes a listing convert:**
1. Lifestyle imagery showing the product in context (in a real kitchen, on a real desk) — white-background alone underperforms in Home & Kitchen
2. Compatibility and sizing specificity in the title — "fits 5-quart Instant Pot Duo" outperforms "pot liner" for long-tail capture
3. Material and durability claims are primary differentiators — buyers read bullet points more carefully here than in impulse categories
4. Bundled accessories listings (e.g., tumbler + lid + straw + cleaning brush) have higher perceived value and outperform single-item listings at similar price points
5. Video in listing: cooking demos, setup tutorials — the category lends itself to demonstration and video conversion lifts are documented (single source: AMZ Prep; no third-party corroboration found)

**Falsifiability flag:** Home & Kitchen is cited as a top category by nearly every Amazon seller research source — which creates a shared-assumption risk. Multiple sources may be citing each other rather than independent data. The category's dominance in raw search volume is well-documented; its profitability for new FBA entrants is less certain and more dependent on subcategory selection than the headline "top category" framing implies.

## Cross-Platform Comparison: What Both Markets Confirm

| Signal | Etsy | Amazon |
|--------|------|--------|
| Personalization premium | Strong — 1 in 3 transactions involves customization | Weaker — Amazon buyers are less likely to wait for custom production |
| Review count as ranking driver | Moderate — search algorithm is tag/SEO weighted | Strong — review velocity is primary ranking signal |
| Mobile-first listing design | 68% of Etsy GMS is mobile | Amazon app is primary discovery surface for 56% of product searches |
| Gifting as primary use case | High in Jewelry, Digital Downloads | Moderate in Beauty, Toys |
| Subscribe & Save / repeat purchase | Not applicable | Critical in Health & Beauty — algorithm rewards it explicitly |
| Social-to-platform discovery | Lower — Etsy search is internal | High — TikTok/Instagram drives pre-qualified traffic to Amazon listings |

---

## Assumptions That Both Markets Share (Unverified)

1. **Review count = quality signal.** Both Etsy and Amazon ecosystems treat high review count as a trust proxy. Neither platform's data directly shows that higher-reviewed products deliver better outcomes for buyers — only that they convert better. This assumption could break if a review-gaming scandal hits either platform.

2. **Personalization = margin premium.** Both markets suggest personalized products command higher prices and loyalty. The underlying mechanism (perceived uniqueness) is logical but the margin data is seller-reported, not audited.

3. **Mobile-first = mobile-optimized listing.** High mobile traffic share (68% Etsy, similar on Amazon) does not directly confirm that listings designed for mobile convert better than desktop-designed listings on mobile — this inference is common but largely unverified.
