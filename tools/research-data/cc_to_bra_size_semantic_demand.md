# CC to Bra Size Calculator — Semantic Demand Research

## Date: 2026-03-20

## Search Queries Analyzed
- "cc to bra size calculator"

- "breast implant sizing confusion" (Reddit)

## Core User Intent
People searching this are overwhelmingly **pre-consultation or between consultations** for breast augmentation. They want to translate the surgeon's language (CCs) into something they understand (bra cup sizes). The mental model gap is the core tension: surgeons speak in volume (cubic centimeters), patients think in cup sizes.

### Confusion Points
1. **Band size matters but nobody explains it** — "Why does the same 350cc look huge on my friend but not on me?" The cc-per-cup-size varies by frame (130cc for a 28 band vs 200cc for a 40 band)
2. **Profile confusion** — Low, moderate, moderate-plus, high profile all look different at the same CC. Users don't understand that 350cc moderate ≠ 350cc high profile in terms of projection
3. **"Natural" vs "augmented" look** — Many users want to know not just what size, but whether it will look natural. The cc number alone doesn't tell this
4. **Cup sizes aren't standardized** — "I'm a C at Victoria's Secret but a D at another store." Bra sizing varies by manufacturer, making the conversion even more confusing
5. **Before/after mental model** — Users think in terms of transformation: "I want to go from X to Y." They need the calculator to speak this language
6. **Existing tissue complicates everything** — "I already have some breast tissue, does that count?" Yes, the implant volume adds to existing volume

### Emotional State
- **Anxious**: This is a major surgery decision, often $6-10K+ investment
- **Confused**: Medical terminology meets fashion sizing meets body image
- **Seeking validation**: "Is this the right size for my body?"
- **Afraid of regret**: "What if I go too big?" / "What if I wish I went bigger?"
- **Comparison-driven**: Looking at before/after photos, trying to match their body type
- **Information-overloaded**: Surgeon consultations dump a lot of data (profile, placement, brand)

## Mental Models
1. **Linear scaling** (WRONG): Users think 2x CCs = 2x bigger. Reality: volume-to-visual is nonlinear and depends on chest width
2. **Universal cup sizes** (WRONG): Users think a C cup is a C cup everywhere. Reality: bra sizing varies wildly across brands
3. **CC = cup** (OVERSIMPLIFIED): The rough "150-200cc per cup size" rule exists but varies significantly by band size
4. **One number fits all** (WRONG): Users want one answer ("350cc = D cup") but it depends on their starting size, band, frame, and implant profile

## Key Facts for Calculator Logic
- **CC per cup size by band:**
  - 28-30 band: ~130-150cc per cup
  - 32 band: ~150-170cc per cup
  - 34 band: ~170-185cc per cup
  - 36 band: ~185-200cc per cup
  - 38-40+ band: ~200-220cc per cup
- **Standard ranges (for reference charts):**
  - A cup: ~130-200cc total
  - B cup: ~200-300cc total
  - C cup: ~300-400cc total
  - D cup: ~400-530cc total
  - DD/E cup: ~530-700cc total
  - F/DDD cup: ~700-850cc total
- **Implant profiles affect projection, not volume:** Same CC looks different across profiles
- **Round vs anatomical (teardrop):** Round is standard reference; anatomical redistributes volume

## Content Gaps in Competing Pages
1. Most calculators don't adjust for band size — they give a flat cc-to-cup chart
2. Nobody explains the "before and after" transformation clearly (current size + implant = new size)
3. No competitor explains profile impact in simple terms
4. The emotional reassurance ("this is normal to be confused") is missing
5. No competitor uses a visual comparison (before → after) that speaks to the transformation mindset
