# Newborn Weight Loss Calculator — Semantic Demand Research

## Date: 2026-03-20

## People Also Ask (Google) [TRACED]
- "How much weight loss is normal for a newborn?" — #1 question. Answer: 5-7% vaginal, up to 10% C-section/breastfed.
- "When should I worry about newborn weight loss?" — Fear-driven. Answer: >10% at any time, not regaining by day 5.
- "When do newborns regain birth weight?" — Timeline question. Answer: by days 10-14.
- "How often should I weigh my newborn?" — Practical. Answer: at birth, hospital, 3-5 day visit.
- "What causes newborn weight loss?" — Understanding. Fluid shifts, limited colostrum, meconium.

## User Emotional State [TRACED from search patterns]
- **Primary emotion:** HIGH ANXIETY. New parents, often sleep-deprived, checking at 2 AM.
- **Mental model:** "My baby is losing weight = something is wrong" — but weight loss in first days is NORMAL and expected.
- **Needs:** Immediate reassurance or clear "call your doctor" signal. No ambiguity.

## The ONE RULE [TRACED from clinical guidelines]
"All newborns lose weight. Up to 7% in the first 3 days is completely normal."

This directly addresses the core anxiety. The number 7% is the key anchor.
