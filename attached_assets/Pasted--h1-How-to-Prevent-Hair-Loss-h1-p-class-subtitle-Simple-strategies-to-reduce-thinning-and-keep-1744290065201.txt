<section id="section-1">
  <h2>Daily Habits That Reduce Hair Loss</h2>
  <p>Healthy routines can make a big difference in how much hair you shed. These simple changes support scalp health and reduce breakage.</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Wash hair regularly with a gentle shampoo</li>
    <li>Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on roots</li>
    <li>Massage your scalp for 5 minutes a day</li>
    <li>Limit heat styling and harsh chemicals</li>
    <li>Use a silk pillowcase to reduce friction</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="section-2">
  <h2>Medical Treatments and Supplements</h2>
  <p>If lifestyle changes aren’t enough, medical support can help. Start with over-the-counter treatments or consult a dermatologist.</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Minoxidil:</strong> FDA-approved topical for both men and women</li>
    <li><strong>Finasteride:</strong> Oral medication for male pattern baldness</li>
    <li><strong>Biotin:</strong> Supports keratin production (often used in hair supplements)</li>
    <li><strong>Iron, Zinc, Vitamin D:</strong> Deficiencies in these nutrients may trigger shedding</li>
    <li><strong>PRP or microneedling:</strong> Procedures that stimulate new growth</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section id="faq">
  <h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
  <div class="faq-item">
    <h4>Can stress really cause hair loss?</h4>
    <p>Yes. Stress-related shedding is called telogen effluvium. It's temporary and usually resolves in a few months.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="faq-item">
    <h4>Does cutting your hair make it grow faster?</h4>
    <p>No. Cutting hair reduces split ends, but growth happens at the scalp — not the ends.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="faq-item">
    <h4>When should I see a doctor?</h4>
    <p>If you're losing large amounts of hair suddenly or see bald patches, book an appointment with a dermatologist.</p>
  </div>
</section>
