      <h2>What Is Ozempic?</h2>
      <p>Ozempic mimics a gut hormone (GLP-1) that suppresses appetite, slows gastric emptying, and improves insulin sensitivity. At higher doses (2.4mg), it's sold as Wegovy and has demonstrated average weight loss of up to 15% in 68 weeks.</p>

      <p>The calculator models results based on these studies and your personal data. But to use it effectively, you need to understand what the output means—and how to make the most of it.</p>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h4>How much weight will I lose on 2.4mg of Ozempic (Wegovy)?</h4>
        <p>Clinical trials show 14.9% average body weight loss after 68 weeks at 2.4mg. That’s ~33 lbs for someone starting at 220 lbs.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h4>How fast will I lose weight?</h4>
        <p>Most users begin to lose within the first 2–4 weeks. Full effects often take 16–20 weeks to show and peak by 68 weeks.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h4>Can I lose more than the calculator predicts?</h4>
        <p>Yes. The calculator reflects average results. Users who exercise, improve diet, and stay consistent often lose more than predicted.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h4>Will I regain the weight after stopping Ozempic?</h4>
        <p>Without lasting lifestyle changes, weight regain is common. Studies show some regain occurs within a year of stopping treatment.</p>
