Common questions

How long does caffeine last in your system?
Caffeine has a half-life of about 5 hours. That means it takes ~5 hours for your body to eliminate half the caffeine. A typical cup of coffee (95 mg) takes 10-15 hours to fully clear below the sleep-disruption threshold.
When should I stop drinking coffee before bed?
Stop caffeine intake at least 8-10 hours before bedtime. For a 10 PM bedtime, your last cup should be by noon to 2 PM. This calculator shows you the exact cutoff based on your drink choice.
How much caffeine is too much?
The FDA recommends no more than 400 mg per day for healthy adults, roughly 4 cups of brewed coffee. Above 50 mg at bedtime is associated with disrupted sleep quality.
Does caffeine half-life change with age?
Yes. Caffeine half-life increases with age and varies with genetics, liver function, pregnancy, and medication use. The standard 5-hour half-life is an average for healthy adults.
