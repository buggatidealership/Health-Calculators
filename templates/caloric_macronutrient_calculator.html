Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this calculator?

The calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which has been shown to be accurate within 10% for most people. However, individual factors like genetics, hormones, and metabolism can affect your actual needs. For best results, use the calculator as a starting point and adjust based on your personal experience.

Should I adjust my macros on rest days?

Some people find benefits in reducing carbohydrate intake on rest days while maintaining protein intake to support recovery. If you're following a specific training protocol, you might consider consuming 10-20% fewer calories on rest days, primarily from carbohydrates, while keeping protein consistent.

How often should I recalculate my needs?

It's recommended to recalculate after any significant body weight change (5+ pounds), when your activity level changes substantially, or every 3-6 months to account for metabolic adaptations. If you're not seeing progress toward your goals after 3-4 weeks, it may also be time to recalculate.
