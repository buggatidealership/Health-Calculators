How Many Calories Are in a Subway Sandwich?

A Subway sandwich ranges from about 200 calories (6-inch Veggie Delite, no sauce) to over 1,100 calories (Footlong Meatball Marinara with cheese and mayo). The biggest calorie drivers are sandwich size (footlong doubles bread + protein), protein choice (tuna and spicy Italian are highest), sauces (mayo adds 110 cal per serving), and cheese (40–50 cal per slice). How to Use the Subway Calorie Calculator

  1. Choose your size — 6-inch or Footlong. The bread and protein values double for a footlong; condiments stay the same.
  2. Select your bread — Bread contributes 200–300 calories. 9-Grain Wheat and Hearty Multigrain add the most fiber.
  3. Pick your protein — This is the biggest variable. Turkey and ham are lean (60 cal); tuna and spicy Italian are dense (190–200 cal).
  4. Add cheese and veggies — Cheese adds 40–50 cal per slice; most veggies are negligible (5–10 cal each).
  5. Choose your sauces — Sauces are the hidden calorie bomb. Mayo adds 110 cal; sweet onion is only 40 cal.
  6. Watch the live totals update as you build your order.

Methodology

This calculator uses per-ingredient nutrition values from Subway's official U.S. Nutrition Information guide (2024) and the USDA FoodData Central database. Bread and protein values are scaled linearly with sandwich size (footlong = 2× the 6-inch values). Cheese, vegetables, sauces, and extras are treated as fixed-portion condiments that do not scale with sandwich size, consistent with standard Subway preparation practices.

Calculations are purely additive — the total calories, protein, carbohydrates, fat, fiber, and sodium represent the sum of all selected components. Sodium values reflect standard formulations and may vary by location. This tool is intended for informational and nutritional planning purposes only. Values may differ from in-store preparation due to regional ingredient variation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many calories are in a Subway footlong?

A Subway footlong ranges from roughly 500 to 1,200+ calories. A Footlong Veggie Delite on 9-Grain Wheat with veggies and mustard is about 440 calories. A Footlong Meatball Marinara with Italian Herbs & Cheese bread, provolone, and mayo can exceed 1,100 calories.

What is the lowest-calorie Subway sandwich?

The lowest-calorie options are the 6-inch Veggie Delite (~210 cal with just bread and veggies), Oven Roasted Turkey (~270 cal), and Black Forest Ham (~270 cal). Choose Italian White or 9-Grain Wheat bread, skip the cheese, load up on veggies, and use mustard or vinegar instead of mayo-based sauces.

Does a footlong have exactly double the calories of a 6-inch?

Almost. Bread and protein scale to exactly double for a footlong. However, condiments (cheese, sauces, extras) are typically applied in similar amounts regardless of sandwich size, so the calorie multiplier for the full sandwich is slightly less than 2× when sauces are included. This calculator accounts for that distinction.

Which Subway sauce has the most calories?

Regular Mayonnaise and Ranch are the highest-calorie sauces at 110 calories per serving each. Chipotle Southwest is close at 100 calories. The lowest-calorie options are Red Wine Vinegar (0 cal), Yellow Mustard (5 cal), and Honey Mustard (30 cal).

How much sodium is in a Subway sandwich?

Subway sandwiches are relatively high in sodium. A 6-inch Rotisserie Chicken on 9-Grain Wheat with standard veggies contains roughly 730–900 mg sodium. The FDA recommends adults consume fewer than 2,300 mg per day, so a footlong can consume a significant portion of the daily limit.

Is Subway healthy?

Subway can be a nutritious choice depending on your order. Turkey and chicken options with vegetables, minimal cheese, and low-calorie sauces provide lean protein and fiber. Processed meat sandwiches (Spicy Italian, Cold Cut Combo) and calorie-dense sauces can quickly turn a sub into a high-fat, high-sodium meal. Use this calculator to stay within your goals.
