About the Waist-to-Hip Ratio

  • Formula: WHR = Waist Circumference ÷ Hip Circumference
  • WHO thresholds: Substantially increased risk at ≥0.90 for men and ≥0.85 for women (WHO Technical Report 894, 2000).
  • Why it matters: WHR captures central (visceral) fat distribution, which is a stronger predictor of cardiovascular events and type 2 diabetes than BMI or weight alone.
  • Measurement tip: Measure waist at the narrowest point (typically at the navel) and hips at the widest point of the buttocks. Use a flexible tape measure, keep it snug but not compressing the skin, and measure while standing.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Men below 0.90, women below 0.80 = low risk (WHO thresholds).

  • WHR measures central fat, which is more strongly linked to heart disease than BMI. INTERHEART study confirms.

  • Apple (central obesity, high WHR) carries greater health risk than pear (peripheral fat, lower WHR).

  • Waist: narrowest point between ribs and hips. Hips: widest point around buttocks.
