What Is Carb Cycling?

Carb cycling is a dietary strategy that alternates between high, moderate, and low carbohydrate intake days throughout the week. The approach aims to optimize glycogen replenishment on training days while promoting fat oxidation on rest days. This calculator uses your TDEE (via the Mifflin-St Jeor equation) and distributes macronutrients based on sports nutrition guidelines from the ISSN.

How to Use the Carb Cycling Calculator

Follow these steps to calculate your personalized carb cycling plan:

  • Enter your personal details: Age, gender, weight, and height to calculate your baseline caloric needs.
  • Select your activity level: This determines your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE).
  • Choose your goal: Fat loss, maintenance, or muscle gain to adjust calorie targets appropriately.
  • Select a carb cycling type: Choose the pattern that matches your preferences and lifestyle.
  • For training-based cycling: Indicate which days of the week you typically train.
  • Click "Calculate": Get a complete weekly breakdown of calories, protein, carbs, and fat.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Carb cycling alternates high, moderate, and low carbohydrate days to optimize glycogen replenishment on training days and fat oxidation on rest days.

  • Yes, it can be effective by manipulating insulin response and optimizing energy use, especially when paired with training schedules.

  • High-carb days are ideal for heavy training. Low-carb days suit rest or light activity.

  • Most people use 1-2 high-carb days, 2-3 medium, and 2 low-carb days weekly depending on training schedule.

  • Protein stays constant at 1.2-1.8g/kg bodyweight across all day types to maintain muscle.

Methodology

TDEE via Mifflin-St Jeor with activity multiplier. Protein held constant at 1.2-1.8 g/kg. Remaining calories split between carbs and fat: High carb day (60% carbs/40% fat), Moderate (40%/60%), Low (20%/80%), Very Low (5%/95%). Training-based option adjusts by workout vs rest days.

Carb cycling alternates carbohydrate intake across the week. High-carb days (60% carbs) align with hard training sessions for glycogen. Low-carb days (20% carbs) promote fat oxidation on rest days. Protein stays constant at 1.2-1.8 g/kg. Calculate TDEE first, then distribute remaining calories after protein. This strategy may improve metabolic flexibility and insulin sensitivity.
