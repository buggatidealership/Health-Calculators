How to Use the Ozempic Weight Loss Calculator

This calculator helps you estimate how much weight you may lose while taking semaglutide (Ozempic or Wegovy), based on real clinical trial data.

  • Weight: Your starting body weight in kg or lbs.
  • Height: Used to calculate your BMI baseline.
  • Dosage: Weekly semaglutide dose (e.g. 0.25mg to 2.4mg).
  • Weeks on Medication: Duration of use — the longer the treatment, the more accurate the projection.
  • Lifestyle: Whether you're making additional dietary and physical activity changes.

These estimates are based on STEP 1–4 trial outcomes and assume consistent medication adherence. Your real-world results may vary.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight will I lose on Ozempic?

Ozempic clinical trials show average weight loss between 5–15% of body weight depending on dose, time, and lifestyle factors. At the 2.4mg dose (Wegovy), average weight loss is approximately 15% after 68 weeks. At lower doses used for diabetes, average weight loss is 5-8%. This calculator provides a personalized estimate based on these clinical averages.

How fast does Ozempic cause weight loss?

Weight loss usually begins after the first few weeks and continues steadily over 12–68 weeks. Weekly average loss varies from 0.3 to 1.0 kg. Most people see noticeable results within 4-8 weeks, with the most rapid weight loss occurring in the first 20 weeks of treatment.

Do I need to change my diet while taking Ozempic?

While Ozempic naturally reduces appetite, clinical trials show that combining the medication with dietary changes and exercise leads to significantly better outcomes. Patients following structured lifestyle programs lost approximately 5% more weight than those on medication alone. Small healthy changes like increasing protein intake and moderate activity can amplify your results.

What happens after I stop Ozempic?

Clinical data shows that approximately two-thirds of weight lost on Ozempic is regained within 1-2 years after stopping the medication unless lifestyle changes are maintained. Long-term studies suggest that continued use may be necessary for many patients to maintain weight loss. Discuss maintenance strategies with your healthcare provider before discontinuing treatment.

Is this calculator based on real research?

Yes. Estimates are based on results from the STEP 1-4 clinical trials published in the New England Journal of Medicine and other medical journals. These trials specifically studied semaglutide (the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy) for weight management. The calculator uses simplified models of this data for educational purposes.
