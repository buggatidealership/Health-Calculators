Frequently Asked Questions

  • Muscle cramps, fatigue, headache, dizziness, heart palpitations, nausea, and brain fog are common signs.

Methodology

This calculator uses NIH Adequate Intake (AI) values stratified by age and sex, with adjustments for exercise sweat losses (~920 mg sodium/L sweat) and diet-specific factors (keto +150% sodium). Sweat volume modeled from activity level, duration, and climate.

Daily electrolyte needs vary by age, sex, activity, climate, and diet. The NIH recommends 1,500 mg sodium, 3,400 mg potassium (men), 400 mg magnesium (men 19-30), and 1,000 mg calcium per day as baseline. Athletes can lose 920+ mg sodium per liter of sweat. Keto diets increase sodium excretion due to lower insulin levels.
