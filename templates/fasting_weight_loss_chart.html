What Factors Influence Weekly Progress?

  • Body Fat %: Leaner individuals lose weight slower than those with high fat mass.
  • Activity Level: Walking or lifting weights during fasting accelerates fat loss.
  • Meal Quality: Clean refeeding keeps progress steady.
  • Sleep & Stress: Hormones matter for fat burning, especially cortisol and insulin.

How to Avoid Stalls After Week 6

Most fasting-related weight loss slows after 6–8 weeks. Here's how to break a plateau:

  • Add 1–2 refeed days weekly (eat at maintenance calories)
  • Track sleep and increase protein intake (at least 1.2g/kg bodyweight)
  • Switch up fasting protocol: try 24-hour fasts, alternate-day fasting, or reduce carbs temporarily

FAQs About Fasting Progress

How much weight can I expect to lose in a month?

Typically 4–8 lbs depending on protocol, consistency, and body size. More aggressive protocols like OMAD yield faster results.

What if I don't lose weight the first week?

It's common to maintain or gain due to water retention or poor tracking. Stay consistent for 2 full weeks before reassessing.

Can I gain muscle while fasting?

Yes, especially during 16:8 if protein is high and resistance training is included. OMAD makes muscle gain harder but not impossible.

Is 20 lbs in 2 months realistic?

Only if you're starting with a high body fat percentage and aggressive fasting + calorie deficit. For most, 12–16 lbs is more realistic.
