How this is calculated

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, recommended by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics as the most accurate for estimating BMR in healthy adults.

Males: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Females: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age) − 161
TDEE = BMR × activity multiplier

TDEE can vary ±200 cal based on genetics, body composition, and metabolic adaptation. Use this as a starting point and adjust based on results over 2–3 weeks.

Mifflin MD et al. Am J Clin Nutr. 1990
