About Cholesterol Ratios

  • Total/HDL Ratio: Total cholesterol divided by HDL. The AHA considers <5 desirable. Below 3.5 is optimal.
  • LDL/HDL Ratio: LDL divided by HDL. Optimal is below 2.0. Values above 3.5 indicate elevated risk.
  • Non-HDL Cholesterol: Total cholesterol minus HDL. Captures all atherogenic lipoproteins (LDL + VLDL). ATP III target: <130 mg/dL for most adults.
  • Triglyceride/HDL Ratio: A proxy marker for insulin resistance and small dense LDL particles. Optimal is below 2.0; above 3.0 suggests metabolic risk.
  • Why ratios matter: Individual cholesterol numbers don't tell the full story. A high total cholesterol with high HDL may be lower risk than a lower total cholesterol with very low HDL.
