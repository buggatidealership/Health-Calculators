Frequently Asked Questions

  • The Body Roundness Index models the body as an ellipse using waist circumference and height. It measures central adiposity, the fat pattern most linked to cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

  • For most people, yes. A 2024 JAMA Network Open study found BRI had a stronger association with all-cause mortality than BMI. BRI measures body shape rather than mass, detecting dangerous visceral fat that BMI misses.

  • BRI 1-3.41 is healthy (low central adiposity). 3.41-4.45 is moderate risk. 4.45-5.73 is high risk. Above 5.73 is very high risk.

Methodology

BRI = 364.2 - 365.5 x sqrt(1 - ((WC/(2*pi))^2 / (0.5*H)^2))

Where WC = waist circumference in meters, H = height in meters. The formula models the body cross-section as an ellipse. Higher BRI = more round body shape = greater central adiposity.

The Body Roundness Index (BRI) uses waist circumference and height to measure central adiposity. BRI 1-3.41 is healthy, 3.41-4.45 moderate risk, 4.45-5.73 high risk, above 5.73 very high risk. A 2024 JAMA Network Open study found BRI outperforms BMI for predicting all-cause mortality. Unlike BMI, BRI does not require weight and cannot misclassify muscular individuals.
