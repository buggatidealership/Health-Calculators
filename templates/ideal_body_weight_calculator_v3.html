Frequently Asked Questions

  • Your ideal weight depends on height, gender, and frame size. Rather than one magic number, four medical formulas provide a range. Being within 10-15% of this range is generally considered healthy. Focus on how you feel and your overall health markers, not a specific number on the scale.

  • No single formula is definitively best. Devine (1974) is most widely used in clinical settings, especially for medication dosing. This calculator averages all four formulas to give you a more balanced picture. The spread between formulas is usually 10-15 pounds.

  • Frame size accounts for bone structure differences. Small frames reduce the target by about 10%, large frames increase it by 10%. Test yours: wrap your thumb and middle finger around your wrist. Overlap means small, touching means medium, a gap means large.

  • They serve different purposes. BMI is simpler but ignores muscle mass and frame. Ideal body weight formulas account for gender and frame size. Neither captures the full picture. Combine them with waist circumference, body fat percentage, and bloodwork for a complete view.

  • Within 10-15% is generally considered healthy. But these formulas were originally designed for medication dosing, not health targets. Your energy, blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar matter more than matching an exact number. A doctor who knows your history gives better guidance than any formula.

Methodology

Four formulas calculate ideal weight based on inches over 5 feet (60 in): Devine: M=50+2.3/in, F=45.5+2.3/in; Robinson: M=52+1.9/in, F=49+1.7/in; Miller: M=56.2+1.41/in, F=53.1+1.36/in; Hamwi: M=48+2.7/in, F=45.5+2.2/in. Frame adjustment: -10% small, +10% large. The displayed range is the average plus/minus 10%.

Ideal body weight is calculated using four medical formulas: Devine (1974), Robinson (1983), Miller (1983), and Hamwi (1964). These formulas use height and gender to estimate the weight associated with lowest disease risk. For a 5'10" male with medium frame, the average across all four formulas is approximately 166 lbs with a healthy range of 149-183 lbs. Frame size adjusts by plus or minus 10%. These formulas were developed for medication dosing and should not be treated as strict health targets.
