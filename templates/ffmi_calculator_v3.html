Frequently Asked Questions

  • For men, FFMI 20-22 is above average (regular training). 22-25 is excellent to superior. For women, 17-19 is above average to excellent.

  • The Kouri et al. (1995) study found no steroid-free athlete exceeded FFMI 25. Pre-steroid-era Mr. America winners topped out at ~25. For women, ~21.

  • FFMI accuracy depends entirely on body fat input. A 3-5% error shifts FFMI by 1-2 points. Use DEXA or hydrostatic weighing for best results.

Sources

  1. Kouri EM, et al. Fat-Free Mass Index in Users and Nonusers of Anabolic-Androgenic Steroids. Clin J Sport Med. 1995;5(4):223-228. PubMed
  2. Schutz Y, et al. Fat-Free Mass Index Percentiles in Caucasians Aged 18-98 y. Int J Obes. 2002;26(7):953-960. PubMed

Methodology

FFM = weight(kg) x (1 - BF%/100)

FFMI = FFM / height(m)^2

Normalized FFMI = FFMI + 6.1 x (1.8 - height_m)

Height correction normalizes to 1.80m reference. Natural limit ~25 for men, ~21 for women (Kouri et al., 1995).

FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index) measures lean muscle mass relative to height. Normalized FFMI adjusts for height. The natural limit is approximately 25 for men and 21 for women, based on the Kouri et al. 1995 study. FFMI 20-22 for men indicates regular weight training. Above 25 is suspicious for steroid use.
