How the Calculation Works

Concentration = Vial Strength (IU) ÷ Bacteriostatic Water (mL)

Injection Volume = Desired Dose (IU) ÷ Concentration (IU/mL)

Syringe Units = Injection Volume (mL) × 100 (on a U-100 insulin syringe)

For example, a 5,000 IU vial reconstituted with 2 mL of bacteriostatic water gives a concentration of 2,500 IU/mL. A 250 IU dose would require 0.10 mL, which is 10 units on a U-100 insulin syringe.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Concentration = vial IU / water mL. Injection volume = prescribed dose / concentration.

  • Most HCG injections use U-100 insulin syringes. 1 mL = 100 units.

  • Refrigerated with bacteriostatic water: typically 30 days. Check product labeling.

  • Bacteriostatic water for multi-dose vials (has preservative). Sterile water for single-dose only.

Methodology

Concentration = Vial IU / Water mL. Volume = Dose / Concentration. Syringe Units = Volume x 100.

HCG dose calculation: divide vial strength by water volume for concentration, then divide prescribed dose by concentration for injection volume.
