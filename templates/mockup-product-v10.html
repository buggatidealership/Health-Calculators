The one rule
Caffeine has a half-life of ~5 hours. That means every 5 hours, your body eliminates half. A 95 mg coffee at 2 PM becomes 47 mg by 7 PM, then 24 mg by midnight. The math is simple. The implication: your afternoon coffee is still in your bloodstream when you go to bed.

Common questions
How long does caffeine last?
Caffeine has a half-life of about 5 hours. A 95 mg cup of coffee takes 10-15 hours to fully clear your system. Most people still have ~24 mg at the 10-hour mark.
When should I stop drinking coffee?
At least 8-10 hours before bedtime. For a 10 PM bedtime, your last cup should be by noon to 2 PM to stay under 25 mg at bedtime.
Does caffeine sensitivity change with age?
Yes. Caffeine half-life tends to increase with age. Older adults metabolize caffeine more slowly, meaning the same cup lasts longer in their system.
What is a safe amount of caffeine per day?
The FDA recommends no more than 400 mg per day for healthy adults, roughly 4 cups of brewed coffee. Sensitivity varies by individual.
