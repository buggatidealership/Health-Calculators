Common questions

What is caffeine's half-life?
Caffeine's half-life is approximately 5 hours for most adults. This means that 5 hours after consumption, half of the caffeine is still in your system. Factors like age, liver function, pregnancy, and certain medications can change this significantly.
How much caffeine is safe per day?
The FDA considers 400mg per day safe for most healthy adults. That's roughly 4 cups of brewed coffee. Pregnant women are typically advised to limit intake to 200mg per day. Individual sensitivity varies widely.
Does caffeine affect sleep quality even below the threshold?
Research suggests that caffeine can reduce deep sleep (slow-wave sleep) even when consumed 6 hours before bed, even if you fall asleep normally. The effect on sleep architecture may not be consciously noticeable but can reduce restorative sleep quality.
Why does caffeine affect people differently?
Genetic variation in the CYP1A2 enzyme determines how quickly you metabolize caffeine. "Fast metabolizers" clear caffeine roughly twice as fast as "slow metabolizers." Smoking speeds up caffeine metabolism; oral contraceptives and pregnancy slow it down.
