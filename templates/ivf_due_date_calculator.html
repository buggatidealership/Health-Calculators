How to Use the IVF Due Date Calculator

Follow these steps to calculate your due date based on your specific IVF treatment:

  1. Select your IVF method: Choose between egg retrieval date, fresh embryo transfer, or frozen embryo transfer (FET).
  2. Enter your procedure date: Select the date of the procedure you chose in the first step.
  3. Specify embryo age: Select whether you had a 3-day embryo, 5-day blastocyst, or 6-day blastocyst transferred.
  4. Calculate: Click the button to see your due date and important pregnancy milestones.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IVF due date different from natural conception?

Slightly. IVF due dates are more precise because transfer day is known. In natural conception, calculations are based on the last menstrual period, which assumes ovulation occurs on day 14. With IVF, we know the exact day the embryo was created or transferred, allowing for more accurate dating.

What's the typical gestation for IVF?

38 weeks from retrieval or 36 weeks from a 5-day transfer. This is because in IVF, clinical pregnancy dating starts from retrieval (equivalent to ovulation), rather than from a last menstrual period. A 40-week pregnancy in natural conception includes approximately 2 weeks before ovulation.

Does embryo age affect due date?

Yes. A 5-day embryo transfer is calculated with a 261-day pregnancy window. The due date calculation adjusts based on embryonic development: 3-day embryos get a longer gestation calculation (266 days from retrieval) than 5-day blastocysts (264 days from retrieval) to account for the additional development that occurred in the lab.

Can I use this if I had FET?

Yes. Enter your frozen embryo transfer date. The calculator will adjust the due date calculation based on the embryo's age at freezing. Frozen embryo transfers typically follow the same gestational timing as fresh transfers of the same embryonic age.

Is this calculator medically accurate?

It's based on accepted obstetric models. Always confirm with your OB-GYN. This calculator uses research-based formulas specific to IVF pregnancies, but individual variations can occur. Your healthcare provider may adjust your due date based on early ultrasound measurements for the most accurate estimate.
