What Is an IVF Due Date?

An IVF due date is calculated from the embryo transfer date rather than the last menstrual period. For a 5-day blastocyst transfer, the estimated due date is the transfer date plus 261 days; for a 3-day embryo transfer, it is the transfer date plus 263 days. This method follows ACOG Committee Opinion No. 700, which states that ART-based dating takes precedence over ultrasound dating.

Medical Disclaimer: This tool provides general educational estimates. For pregnancy confirmation and prenatal care, consult a licensed OB-GYN.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IVF due date different from natural conception?

Yes. IVF dates are more precise because the day of fertilization or transfer is known exactly.

What's the typical IVF gestation?

About 38 weeks from egg retrieval or 36 weeks from a 5-day transfer.

Does embryo age affect the due date?

Yes. A 3-day embryo results in a longer pregnancy window than a 5-day or 6-day blastocyst.

Can I use this for frozen embryo transfers?

Yes. Choose "Frozen Transfer" and enter your transfer date + embryo age.

Is this calculator medically accurate?

It uses standard IVF dating formulas but should not replace medical advice or ultrasound-based estimates.
