How Much Do You Need to Retire?

Retirement savings planning uses the compound interest formula to project how your current savings and regular contributions will grow over time. This calculator applies the future value formula with periodic contributions and adjusts for inflation. For withdrawal planning, it uses the 4% safe withdrawal rate from the Trinity Study (1998), which analyzed 30-year retirement portfolios across historical market conditions.

How This Calculator Works

  • Compound interest is applied monthly based on expected return.
  • Contributions grow over time with reinvested earnings.
  • Inflation is not included (assumes nominal returns).
  • Results are pre-tax and assume consistent contributions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this calculator?

It gives an estimate based on consistent contributions and growth rate.

Does it account for inflation?

No, values are shown in today's dollars (nominal).

Can I include Social Security or pension income?

Not in this version. It focuses solely on savings accumulation.

What return rate should I use?

A conservative estimate is 5–7% for balanced portfolios.

What happens if I miss contributions?

Your actual savings may be lower than estimated.

Methodology

This calculator projects retirement savings using the future value of an annuity formula with compound interest. Your current savings grow at the assumed annual return rate (7% nominal, compounded monthly), while monthly contributions are added and compounded over the remaining years until retirement. The total projected balance is the sum of the future value of your current savings and the future value of all periodic contributions. For withdrawal planning, the calculator applies the 4% rule derived from the Trinity Study (Cooley, Hubbard, and Walz, 1998), which analyzed historical 30-year portfolio survival rates to determine a sustainable annual withdrawal rate.

Key limitations: the 7% return rate is a long-term historical average for diversified stock portfolios and is not guaranteed. Returns are shown in nominal (pre-inflation) terms, though an inflation-adjusted estimate using 3% annual inflation is also provided. The model assumes consistent monthly contributions with no interruptions, no taxes on withdrawals, and no changes to contribution amounts over time. Actual results will vary based on market performance, tax situation, and changes in contributions.
