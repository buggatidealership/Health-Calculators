Wegovy vs Ozempic: Same Drug, Different Purpose

Wegovy and Ozempic both contain semaglutide, a GLP-1 receptor agonist made by Novo Nordisk. The critical difference is FDA indication and dosing. Wegovy is approved for chronic weight management in adults with BMI 30+ (or 27+ with a weight-related condition) at a maximum dose of 2.4 mg weekly. Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes at up to 2.0 mg weekly. The STEP trials (Wegovy) specifically measured weight loss as the primary endpoint, while SUSTAIN trials (Ozempic) focused on blood sugar control with weight loss as a secondary finding.

Wegovy 2.4 mg (injectable): Average 14.9% body weight loss at 68 weeks (STEP 1, N=1,961)

Wegovy oral 50 mg (pill): Approximately 15% body weight loss at 68 weeks (OASIS trials)

Ozempic 1.0 mg: Average ~8% body weight loss at 52 weeks (SUSTAIN trials)

Ozempic 0.5 mg: Average ~5% body weight loss at 52 weeks (SUSTAIN trials)

Because Wegovy reaches a higher maintenance dose (2.4 mg vs 2.0 mg) and was studied in weight-focused populations, its clinical weight loss data is substantially stronger than Ozempic's. If your primary goal is weight management rather than blood sugar control, Wegovy's trial data is the more relevant reference point.

How the Calculator Works

This calculator uses weight loss percentages from the STEP and OASIS clinical trial programs. It factors in your starting weight, BMI, treatment form (injectable or oral), treatment duration, and activity level to estimate total weight loss. The projection uses a non-linear logarithmic curve that models the typical pattern of rapid initial loss followed by gradual plateau.

Clinical Trial Data Sources

Injectable (2.4 mg weekly): STEP 1 trial — 14.9% average loss at 68 weeks. About one-third of participants lost 20% or more. Weight loss was progressive through 68 weeks with the steepest decline between weeks 12-28 during dose escalation.

Oral pill (50 mg daily): OASIS trials — approximately 15% average loss at 68 weeks. The oral formulation showed comparable efficacy to injectable semaglutide, with a daily dosing schedule rather than weekly injections.

Activity Level Adjustment

The STEP trials required participants to maintain 150 minutes/week of moderate physical activity. Our calculator adjusts the projection slightly based on your reported activity level: sedentary users may see somewhat less weight loss than clinical averages, while highly active users may exceed them. This adjustment is modest (up to +/-10% of the base projection) because the medication effect dominates the exercise effect in clinical data.

Wegovy Dose Titration Schedule

Wegovy uses a mandatory 16-week dose escalation to minimize gastrointestinal side effects (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea). You should not skip steps or accelerate the schedule without medical supervision.

Injectable Schedule

Weeks 1-4: 0.25 mg once weekly

Weeks 5-8: 0.5 mg once weekly

Weeks 9-12: 1.0 mg once weekly

Weeks 13-16: 1.7 mg once weekly

Week 17 onward: 2.4 mg once weekly (maintenance dose)

Oral Pill Schedule

Weeks 1-4: 3 mg once daily

Weeks 5-8: 7 mg once daily

Weeks 9-12: 14 mg once daily

Weeks 13-16: 25 mg once daily

Week 17 onward: 50 mg once daily (maintenance dose)

During the dose-titration phase (weeks 1-16), the primary goal is building tolerability. Significant weight loss typically begins after reaching the maintenance dose, though most patients begin losing weight during escalation. The calculator accounts for the reduced dosing during this initial period.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much weight can I lose on Wegovy?

In the STEP 1 clinical trial (N=1,961), participants taking Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly) lost an average of 14.9% of their body weight over 68 weeks, compared to 2.4% with placebo. Approximately one-third of participants achieved 20% or greater weight loss. In the longer STEP 5 trial, weight loss was maintained at 15.2% over 104 weeks (2 years). Results depend on treatment duration, adherence, baseline BMI, diet, and exercise habits.

What is the difference between Wegovy and Ozempic?

Both contain semaglutide, but Wegovy is FDA-approved specifically for chronic weight management at a higher dose (2.4 mg weekly), while Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes management at up to 2.0 mg weekly. Wegovy's STEP trials enrolled adults with obesity (BMI 30+) regardless of diabetes status and measured weight loss as the primary endpoint. Ozempic's SUSTAIN trials focused on glycemic control. Additionally, Wegovy now has an FDA-approved oral pill form (50 mg daily), while Ozempic does not have an oral weight-loss formulation.

Is there an oral Wegovy pill?

Yes. In 2025, the FDA approved an oral semaglutide formulation for chronic weight management. The OASIS clinical trials demonstrated that oral semaglutide 50 mg taken daily achieved approximately 15% weight loss at 68 weeks — comparable to the injectable 2.4 mg weekly formulation. The oral pill must be taken on an empty stomach with a small amount of water, at least 30 minutes before food or other medications, to ensure proper absorption.

When will I start seeing results on Wegovy?

Most people start noticing weight loss within 4-6 weeks of starting treatment. In the STEP trials, participants showed measurable weight reduction by week 4, with weight loss continuing progressively through week 68. The rate of loss is typically fastest between weeks 12 and 28 during and after dose escalation. During the initial titration phase (weeks 1-16), the primary goal is tolerability, but most patients begin losing weight during this period.

Will I regain weight after stopping Wegovy?

The STEP 4 trial specifically studied this question. Participants who switched from semaglutide 2.4 mg to placebo after 20 weeks of treatment regained approximately two-thirds of the weight they had lost over the following 48 weeks. They also experienced increases in waist circumference, HbA1c, and lipid levels. Maintaining diet and exercise habits after discontinuation can reduce the extent of regain, but most people experience some weight rebound because the appetite-suppressing and metabolic effects of semaglutide cease when the medication is stopped.

Is this calculator based on real clinical trials?

Yes. The weight loss projections are derived from the STEP (Semaglutide Treatment Effect in People with obesity) and OASIS clinical trial programs. STEP 1 was published in the New England Journal of Medicine (Wilding et al., 2021, N=1,961), STEP 2 in The Lancet (Davies et al., 2021, N=1,210), STEP 3 in JAMA (Wadden et al., 2021, N=611), and STEP 5 provided 2-year data (Garvey et al., 2022). The OASIS trials evaluated the oral semaglutide formulation. These trials represent the strongest available evidence for semaglutide weight loss outcomes at the Wegovy dose level.

Methodology

This calculator projects weight loss using clinical trial data from two sources. For injectable Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly), projections are based on the STEP 1 trial (Wilding et al., NEJM 2021, N=1,961), which demonstrated 14.9% average body weight loss at 68 weeks. For oral Wegovy (semaglutide 50 mg daily), projections use data from the OASIS clinical trials showing approximately 15% weight loss at 68 weeks. The calculator applies a non-linear logarithmic curve to model the typical clinical pattern of rapid initial weight loss followed by gradual plateau. Activity level adjustments modify the projection by up to +/-10% based on user-reported exercise habits, since all STEP trial participants received standardized lifestyle counseling (150 minutes/week of physical activity).

Projections represent population averages from controlled clinical trials. Individual results vary significantly based on adherence, diet quality, genetics, metabolic health, and baseline BMI. The calculator does not account for dose titration timing, treatment interruptions, or the presence of type 2 diabetes (STEP 2 showed reduced weight loss of 9.6% in patients with T2D). This tool provides estimates only and does not replace medical advice from a healthcare provider.
