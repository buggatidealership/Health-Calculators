Methodology

This calculator converts breast implant volume in cubic centimeters (cc) to estimated post-operative bra cup size using volumetric data from plastic surgery research. The conversion rate is adjusted based on band size:

new_cups = current_cups + (implant_cc / cc_per_cup)

Where cc_per_cup ranges from 130cc for a 28-band to 210cc for a 42-band, calibrated against 3D CT imaging studies (Pfeiffer et al., 2017) and clinical augmentation data (Tebbetts & Adams, 2005).

Bra sizing is not standardized across manufacturers, and cup size perception varies with band size, implant profile, placement (subglandular vs. submuscular), and existing breast tissue. This tool provides a general approximation only. Final sizing decisions should be made in consultation with a board-certified plastic surgeon using dimensional planning and sizers.

Breast implant volume in CCs converts to cup sizes at approximately 150-200cc per cup size increase, depending on band size. A 34B with 350cc implants typically becomes a 34D. Smaller frames (28-30 band) need less volume per cup (about 130-150cc), while larger frames (38-40 band) need more (about 200-220cc). The conversion also depends on implant profile (low, moderate, high), placement (over or under the muscle), and existing breast tissue. These are estimates only; consult a board-certified plastic surgeon for personalized sizing.
