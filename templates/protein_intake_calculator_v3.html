Frequently Asked Questions

  • For healthy individuals with normal kidney function, intakes up to 2.2 g/kg/day are well-supported. A 2016 review found no adverse effects at intakes up to 3.3 g/kg/day in trained individuals.

  • Total daily protein intake matters more than timing. Distributing protein across 3-4 meals (0.4-0.55 g/kg per meal) maximizes muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

Methodology

This calculator applies a grams-per-kilogram multiplier to body weight based on activity level and goal. Ranges are from the ISSN (2017) and ACSM: 0.8-1.0 g/kg for general health, 1.2-1.6 g/kg for fat loss, 1.6-2.0 g/kg for muscle building. For adults over 65, the PROT-AGE recommendations apply (1.0-1.2 g/kg baseline).

Daily protein intake depends on weight, activity, and goal. The RDA minimum is 0.8 g/kg for sedentary adults, but active individuals need 1.4-2.0 g/kg/day (ISSN). For muscle building, aim for 1.6-2.0 g/kg/day. For fat loss with muscle preservation, 1.2-1.6 g/kg/day. Distribute across 3-4 meals with 0.4-0.55 g/kg per meal. Adults over 65 need at least 1.0-1.2 g/kg/day due to age-related anabolic resistance.
