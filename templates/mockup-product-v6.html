The One Rule

Caffeine follows a simple exponential decay. Every 5 hours, your body eliminates half the remaining caffeine. This isn't an approximation — it's the pharmacokinetic model used in clinical research. A 95 mg cup at 8 AM becomes 47 mg by 1 PM, 24 mg by 6 PM, and 12 mg by 11 PM.

Why It Matters

Sleep researchers define "caffeine-free sleep" as having less than 25 mg in your system at bedtime. Above that threshold, caffeine reduces deep sleep duration even if you fall asleep normally. You don't feel wired — you just don't recover.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How long does caffeine stay in your system?
    Caffeine has a half-life of about 5 hours. A typical cup of coffee (95 mg) takes 10-15 hours to drop below perceptible levels.
  • When should I stop drinking coffee before bed?
    At least 8-10 hours before bedtime. For a 10 PM bedtime, your last cup should be by noon to 2 PM.
  • Does caffeine half-life vary between people?
    Yes. Genetics, age, liver function, pregnancy, and some medications can shift half-life from 3 to 7+ hours. This calculator uses the clinical average of 5 hours.
  • Is 400 mg per day really safe?
    The FDA recommends no more than 400 mg/day for healthy adults. The issue isn't total intake — it's timing. 400 mg at 8 AM is very different from 400 mg at 3 PM.
