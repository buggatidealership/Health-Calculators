Methodology

This calculator generates a composite risk score (0-100) based on five weighted factors from clinical dermatology research: family history pattern (weighted highest at 25 pts per level, reflecting ~80% heritability), current hair status via the Hamilton-Norwood scale (10 pts per level), observed rate of loss (5 pts per level), chronic stress (3 pts per level), and smoking status (4 pts per level).

The predicted balding age is estimated by extrapolating current progression rates. Low risk (0-30): 10-20 years per Norwood stage. Moderate risk (30-60): 5-10 years per stage. High risk (60-100): 2-5 years per stage. Individual results may vary significantly. Consult a dermatologist for clinical evaluation.

Male pattern baldness (androgenetic alopecia) is a polygenic condition influenced by over 389 genetic loci. Risk is determined primarily by family history (80% heritability), current hair status on the Norwood scale, rate of progression, stress level, and smoking status. By age 50, approximately half of men show visible hair loss. Treatments like finasteride and minoxidil can slow progression when started early. This calculator uses a weighted composite scoring model derived from GWAS studies and clinical progression data to estimate individual risk.
