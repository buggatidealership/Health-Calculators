Frequently Asked Questions

  • Most newborns lose 5-7% of birth weight in the first few days. Breastfed babies may lose up to 10% because mature milk takes 2-5 days to come in. This is completely normal. Weight loss beyond 10% at any time needs medical evaluation.

  • Contact your pediatrician if: weight loss exceeds 10%, baby isn't gaining by day 5, fewer than 6 wet diapers per day after day 4, persistent lethargy, or not back to birth weight by day 14. These could indicate feeding problems that need prompt evaluation.

  • Most healthy newborns regain birth weight by 10-14 days. Weight loss typically bottoms out by day 3-4, then reverses. Formula-fed babies tend to regain slightly faster. If birth weight isn't regained by 2 weeks, consult your pediatrician.

  • Normal causes: fluid shifts (babies are born with extra fluid), limited colostrum before mature milk, and meconium passage. C-section babies may lose more because of extra IV fluids during delivery. Most causes are temporary and expected.

  • Yes. Breastfed babies typically lose 7-10% because mature milk takes days to arrive. Formula-fed babies usually lose 5-7%. Both are normal. If you are breastfeeding and concerned, a lactation consultant can check latch and milk transfer.

Methodology

This calculator computes weight loss as a percentage of birth weight: (birth - current) / birth * 100. Results are compared against AAP thresholds: up to 7% is normal for vaginal delivery, up to 10% for breastfed/C-section babies. The tool uses simplified percentage cutoffs rather than the full NEWT hour-by-hour nomograms.

Newborn weight loss of 5-7% is normal in the first 3-4 days of life. Breastfed babies may lose up to 10%. This happens because babies are born with extra fluid and colostrum comes in small amounts before mature milk arrives on days 2-5. Most babies regain birth weight by days 10-14. Weight loss exceeding 10% of birth weight requires medical evaluation. The NEWT (Newborn Weight Loss Tool) provides hour-by-hour percentile curves to identify excessive loss.
