Frequently Asked Questions

  • MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) measures energy cost of activities as a multiple of resting metabolic rate. MET of 1.0 = sitting quietly. Running at 6 mph has MET of 9.8, meaning it burns 9.8x more energy than resting.

  • MET-based calculations are accurate within 10-20% for most people. Actual calories depend on fitness level, body composition, and environmental conditions.

  • Yes, significantly. Heavier individuals burn more calories performing the same activity because more energy is needed to move greater mass. A 90 kg person burns about 50% more than a 60 kg person for the same exercise.

  • Sprinting (MET 23.0), jump rope (MET 12.3), and fast running (MET 11.8) burn the most per minute. But sustainability matters more than peak intensity for total calorie burn.

  • For weight loss, maintain your deficit and eat back 50-75% of burned calories. For maintenance or muscle gain, replacing exercise calories helps fuel recovery.

Methodology

This calculator uses the formula: Calories = MET x Weight (kg) x Duration (hours). MET values are from the 2011 Compendium of Physical Activities (Ainsworth et al.). One MET equals approximately 1 kcal/kg/hour.

Calories burned during exercise are estimated using MET values. The formula is: Calories = MET x body weight (kg) x duration (hours). Running at 6 mph (MET 9.8) for 30 minutes burns approximately 343 calories for a 70 kg person. Higher MET values mean more calories per minute. The Compendium of Physical Activities catalogs MET values for over 800 activities.
