About Gestational Age Calculation

  • Naegele's rule: Due date = LMP + 280 days (40 weeks). This assumes a 28-day menstrual cycle with ovulation on day 14.
  • Gestational vs. fetal age: Gestational age counts from the first day of the last menstrual period. Fetal age (conceptional age) counts from fertilization — typically 2 weeks less than gestational age.
  • Ultrasound dating: First-trimester ultrasound (before 14 weeks) is the most accurate dating method, with accuracy of ±5-7 days. It measures crown-rump length (CRL) to estimate gestational age.
  • IVF dating: The most precise method because the exact date of fertilization/transfer is known. Gestational age = transfer date minus (14 + embryo age at transfer).

Frequently Asked Questions

  • From first day of last menstrual period (LMP). Includes ~2 weeks before conception. Due date = LMP + 280 days.

  • Fetal age = gestational age minus 14 days. Gestational age is the standard used in obstetrics.

  • Only 5% of babies born on due date. First-trimester ultrasound accurate within 5-7 days.

Methodology

Days since LMP-equivalent date. For ultrasound: back-calculated from reported age. For IVF: LMP = transfer date - (14 + embryo days). Due date: LMP + 280 days.
