Methodology

This calculator estimates weight loss during intermittent fasting using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation for Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR):

Males: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) - (5 × age) + 5

Females: BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) - (5 × age) - 161

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is BMR multiplied by an activity factor (1.2-1.9). For daily protocols (16:8, 20:4, OMAD), the caloric deficit is estimated assuming approximately 70% of normal hourly caloric intake during the eating window. For extended fasts (24+ hours), the full TDEE is the deficit.

Fat loss uses the standard 3,500 kcal per pound (7,700 kcal per kg) approximation. Water weight from glycogen depletion is estimated separately: up to 0.5 kg for the first 24 hours, with diminishing additional loss over days 2-3 (max ~0.8 kg). Week-by-week projections assume consistent adherence and include metabolic adaptation of approximately 5% reduction per month.

Intermittent fasting weight loss depends on your fasting protocol, body weight, and activity level. A 170-pound person doing 16:8 fasting (skipping breakfast) can expect to lose approximately 1-2 pounds per week, or 4-8 pounds in the first month. The first week includes 1-3 pounds of water weight from glycogen depletion. Longer protocols like OMAD (one meal a day) or 48-hour fasts create larger deficits but are harder to sustain. The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is used to estimate BMR, multiplied by an activity factor for TDEE. Fat loss is calculated at 3,500 calories per pound of body fat. For sustainable results, most nutrition researchers recommend 16:8 or 20:4 protocols combined with resistance training to preserve muscle mass.
