Frequently Asked Questions

  • Canine pregnancy averages 63 days from ovulation, with a normal window of 58-68 days from mating.

  • Temperature drop below 99F (37.2C) 12-24 hours before delivery, restlessness, nesting, decreased appetite, and visible contractions.

  • Small breeds may deliver slightly earlier (~62 days), giant breeds slightly later (64-65 days). Individual variation is common.

Sources

  1. Johnston SD, Root Kustritz MV, Olson PNS. Canine and Feline Theriogenology. Saunders; 2001.
  2. Concannon PW. Reproductive cycles of the domestic bitch. Anim Reprod Sci. 2011;124(3-4):200-210. PubMed

Dog pregnancy (gestation) averages 63 days from ovulation. The normal whelping window is 58-68 days from mating. Small breeds may deliver around day 62, giant breeds around day 64-65. Key signs of imminent labor: rectal temperature drops below 99F, nesting behavior, decreased appetite. Always consult a veterinarian for prenatal care.
