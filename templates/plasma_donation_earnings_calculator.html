How Much Can You Earn Donating Plasma?

Plasma donation compensation typically ranges from $30-70 per visit, with new donor bonuses up to $100 per visit for the first 6-8 donations. The FDA allows up to two donations within a 7-day period with at least one day between sessions. Frequently Asked Questions

How much does plasma donation pay?

Plasma donation typically pays between $20-60 per donation for regular donors, and $40-100 for new donors. Compensation varies based on weight, location, frequency, and promotional bonuses.

How often can I donate plasma?

FDA regulations allow plasma donation up to twice in a 7-day period, with at least one day between donations. You can donate up to 104 times per year, though most donors give 1-2 times per week.

Do heavier people get paid more for plasma?

Yes. Donors weighing over 175 lbs typically receive $5-15 more per donation because they can safely donate a larger volume of plasma.

What factors affect plasma donation pay?

Compensation depends on your weight, donation center location, frequency of donation, first-time donor status, and special promotions. Our calculator accounts for all these variables.

Do I qualify to donate plasma?

Basic qualifications include being 18-65 years old (up to 75 at some centers), weighing at least 110 lbs, and being in good health. You'll need valid ID and proof of address. Some medical conditions, medications, and recent tattoos/piercings may temporarily disqualify you.

How long does a plasma donation take?

First-time donors should expect the process to take 2-3 hours due to additional screening. Return visits typically take 1-1.5 hours, with the actual donation process taking about 45 minutes.

Where do plasma centers pay the most?

California, New York, and Texas generally offer the highest plasma donation compensation, while states with lower costs of living like North Dakota and Wyoming typically offer lower rates. Urban areas with multiple competing centers often have better compensation.

How is plasma donation payment calculated?

Payment is typically based on volume collected, which correlates with donor weight. Centers offer baseline rates plus bonuses for first-time donors, consistent donation frequency, and special promotions. Rates are adjusted for location-based factors like cost of living and competition.
