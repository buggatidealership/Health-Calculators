Methodology

This calculator uses per-ingredient nutrition values from Subway's official U.S. Nutrition Information guide (2024) and the USDA FoodData Central database. Bread and protein values scale linearly with sandwich size (footlong = 2x the 6-inch values). Cheese, vegetables, sauces, and extras are fixed-portion condiments that do not scale with size.

Calculations are purely additive. Sodium values reflect standard formulations and may vary by location. This tool is for informational and nutritional planning purposes only.

A typical Subway 6-inch sandwich contains 200-450 calories depending on protein choice. Turkey and ham are the leanest at ~270 cal. Meatball Marinara is highest at ~450 cal. Sauces are the hidden calorie bomb: mayo adds 110 cal per serving. A footlong doubles the bread and protein calories but keeps condiments the same. To stay under 400 calories on a 6-inch: choose turkey or ham, skip the cheese, load veggies, and use mustard instead of mayo.
