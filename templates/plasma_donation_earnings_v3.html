Methodology

This calculator estimates plasma donation earnings using publicly available compensation data from major plasma collection centers (BioLife, CSL Plasma, Grifols, Octapharma) as of early 2026.

Compensation is calculated using: Net Earnings = (Base Rate + Weight Bonus + New Donor Bonus) × Donations per Period − Travel Costs

Base rates are adjusted by geographic region (cost-of-living factor), donor weight category (175+ lbs qualifies for higher volume collection and higher pay), and new donor promotional bonuses (typically the first 6-8 donations). The effective hourly rate divides net earnings per visit by average appointment duration (75 minutes for return visits, 150 minutes for first visits).

Actual compensation varies significantly between individual donation centers, promotional periods, and market conditions. The estimates represent typical ranges rather than guaranteed rates.

Plasma donation pays $30-75 per visit for regular donors and $50-100+ for new donors. The FDA allows up to two donations per week with at least one day between sessions. Heavier donors (175+ lbs) earn more because they donate a larger volume of plasma. Donating twice weekly, a regular donor can earn approximately $260-650 per month, or $3,100-7,800 per year. New donor bonuses can add $200-1,000+ in the first month. The effective hourly rate works out to roughly $20-50/hour based on a 75-minute average return visit. Pay varies by location, with California, New York, and Texas generally paying the most.
