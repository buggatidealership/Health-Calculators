How This Calculator Works

This calculator uses evidence-based volume landmarks from peer-reviewed research to generate personalized recommendations. Here's what drives each number:

Base Volume (from Israetel & Schoenfeld research)

Each muscle group has a research-backed volume range defined by three landmarks:

  • MEV (Minimum Effective Volume) — the fewest sets per week that still produce measurable growth
  • MAV (Maximum Adaptive Volume) — the "sweet spot" where most growth occurs relative to effort
  • MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume) — the ceiling above which recovery fails and progress reverses

Your recommended sets target the midpoint of MAV, adjusted for your inputs.

Recovery Modifiers

Base volume is scaled by three recovery factors:

  • Age: Recovery capacity declines with age due to reduced satellite cell availability and slower muscle protein synthesis. Volume is scaled from 100% (18–30) down to 70% (60+), based on Angleri et al. (2024).
  • Sleep: Sleep deprivation reduces muscle protein synthesis by ~18% and elevates cortisol by ~21% (Lamon et al., 2021). Fair sleep applies a 0.85x modifier; poor sleep applies 0.70x.
  • Stress: Chronic stress elevates cortisol, blunting hypertrophic adaptation. High stress applies a 0.80x modifier.

Per-Session Distribution

Research shows diminishing returns beyond ~10 hard sets per muscle per session (Schoenfeld et al., 2016). Weekly volume is distributed across your training days with a cap of 10 sets per muscle per session. If your target volume exceeds what your frequency can handle, the calculator recommends increasing frequency.

What Counts as a "Set"

A "set" means a working set taken to RPE 7+ (1–3 reps in reserve). Warm-up sets, sets with less than 40% 1RM, and sets stopped well short of failure do not count toward these targets.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • 10-20 hard sets per week is optimal for most people. Beginners grow with 8-12, advanced may need 16-22 sets.

  • Frequency is primarily a tool for distributing volume. Training a muscle 2x vs 3x per week shows no significant difference when total weekly sets are equal.

  • A working set at RPE 7+ (1-3 reps in reserve). Warm-ups and sets well short of failure don't count.

  • Recovery capacity declines with age. Reduce volume by 10-30% compared to younger lifters while maintaining intensity.

  • Sleep deprivation reduces muscle protein synthesis by ~18%. Reduce volume by 15-30% during periods of poor sleep.

Methodology

Uses volume landmarks (MEV, MAV, MRV) from Israetel and Schoenfeld research. Base volume adjusted by experience level (beginner/intermediate/advanced), goal (hypertrophy/strength/general), age modifier (100% at 18-30 down to 70% at 60+), sleep quality (100%/85%/70%), and stress (100%/92%/80%). Per-session cap at 10 sets per muscle.

Optimal training volume is 10-20 hard sets per muscle group per week for most people. Beginners: 8-12 sets, intermediate: 14-18, advanced: 16-22. A 'hard set' means RPE 7+ (1-3 reps in reserve). Train each muscle at least 2x/week. Cap at 10 sets per muscle per session. Sleep deprivation reduces muscle protein synthesis by 18%. Recovery capacity declines with age -- reduce volume 10-30% for older adults.
