GI vs. GL: Why Both Matter

Glycemic Index (GI) measures how quickly a food raises blood sugar compared to pure glucose (GI = 100). But GI alone can be misleading because it doesn't account for portion size.

Glycemic Load (GL) solves this by factoring in how many carbs you actually eat: GL = (GI × grams of carbs) ÷ 100. This is why watermelon has a high GI (76) but a low GL (8 per cup) — it's mostly water with few carbs per serving.

For practical dietary decisions, GL is more useful than GI alone.
