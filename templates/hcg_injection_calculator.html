How Reconstitution Works

HCG comes as a freeze-dried powder that must be mixed with bacteriostatic water before injection. The amount of water you add determines the concentration:

  • Concentration = Vial strength (IU) ÷ Water volume (mL)
  • Injection volume = Desired dose (IU) ÷ Concentration (IU/mL)

More water = lower concentration = larger (easier to measure) injection volumes. Less water = higher concentration = smaller volumes. For small doses (250-500 IU), using more water (5-10 mL) makes accurate measurement easier.

How to Calculate HCG Injection Dosage

To calculate your HCG injection volume: divide your prescribed dose (IU) by the concentration (IU/mL). The concentration is determined by how much bacteriostatic water you add to the vial. For example, a 5,000 IU vial mixed with 2 mL of water gives a concentration of 2,500 IU/mL. A 500 IU dose would then require 0.20 mL (20 units on a U-100 insulin syringe).

How to Mix HCG: Step by Step

  1. Gather supplies: HCG vial (powder), bacteriostatic water vial, alcohol swabs, syringe for mixing (3 mL syringe with 18-21 gauge needle), and insulin syringe for injecting (29-31 gauge).
  2. Clean vial tops: Swab both the HCG vial and bacteriostatic water vial tops with alcohol pads. Let dry.
  3. Draw water: Using the mixing syringe, draw the prescribed amount of bacteriostatic water (e.g. 2 mL).
  4. Inject into HCG vial: Insert the needle into the HCG vial and slowly inject the water. Aim the stream at the glass wall, not directly onto the powder.
  5. Gently mix: Roll the vial between your palms for 30-60 seconds. Do not shake — shaking can damage the HCG molecules. The solution should become clear.
  6. Label the vial: Write the date of reconstitution and the concentration on the vial. Store in the refrigerator.
  7. Draw your dose: Using an insulin syringe, draw the calculated volume for your prescribed dose.

Storage & Handling

  • Before reconstitution: Store the powder vial at room temperature (up to 25°C / 77°F). Protect from light.
  • After reconstitution: Refrigerate immediately at 2-8°C (36-46°F). Use within 30-60 days per your pharmacist's instructions.
  • Do not freeze reconstituted HCG.
  • Discard if the solution is cloudy, discolored, or contains particles.
  • Use bacteriostatic water (contains benzyl alcohol preservative), not sterile water, if the vial will be used for multiple doses. Sterile water has no preservative and risks contamination with repeated needle entries.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much bacteriostatic water should I add to HCG?

There is no single "correct" amount — it depends on your prescribed dose. The key principle: more water = lower concentration = larger injection volumes that are easier to measure. For small doses (250-500 IU), 5-10 mL of water is practical because it produces measurable volumes. For larger doses (5,000-10,000 IU as a single injection), 1-2 mL is typical. Follow your prescriber's specific instructions.

What do "units" on an insulin syringe mean?

A standard U-100 insulin syringe has 100 units per 1 mL. So 10 units = 0.10 mL, 25 units = 0.25 mL, 50 units = 0.50 mL, and 100 units = 1.00 mL. These "units" refer to volume markings, not IU of HCG. The number of IU per syringe unit depends entirely on how you mixed your vial.

How long does reconstituted HCG last?

When mixed with bacteriostatic water and refrigerated, HCG typically lasts 30-60 days. Some pharmacies label it for 30 days to be conservative. If mixed with plain sterile water (no preservative), use within 24-48 hours. Always check your pharmacy's specific instructions. Discard if cloudy or past the expiration date.

What is the typical HCG dose for fertility?

For ovulation trigger shots (IVF or timed intercourse), the standard dose is 5,000-10,000 IU as a single injection, given 34-36 hours before egg retrieval or intercourse. Some protocols use 5,000 IU for patients at risk of ovarian hyperstimulation. Pre-filled Ovidrel (250 mcg, approximately 5,000-6,000 IU equivalent) does not require reconstitution. Always follow your fertility specialist's protocol.

Can I use this calculator for peptide reconstitution?

The math is the same for any reconstituted peptide: concentration = total amount ÷ water volume, and injection volume = desired dose ÷ concentration. However, peptides use micrograms (mcg) rather than IU. We have a dedicated Semaglutide Reconstitution Calculator for GLP-1 peptides.
