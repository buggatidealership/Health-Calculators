Methodology

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (or Katch-McArdle when body fat is provided) to compute BMR, then compares it to sex-specific reference BMR values at ages 20-70.

Men: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age + 5

Women: BMR = 10 x weight(kg) + 6.25 x height(cm) - 5 x age - 161

The age whose reference BMR most closely matches yours is your metabolic age.

Metabolic age is determined by comparing your basal metabolic rate (BMR) to population averages. If your BMR is higher than the average for your chronological age, your metabolic age is younger. The Mifflin-St Jeor equation predicts BMR within 10% for most adults. Building lean muscle through resistance training is the most effective way to lower metabolic age.
