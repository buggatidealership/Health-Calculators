About the hCG Doubling Formula

  • Formula: Doubling Time = (Hours Between Tests × ln(2)) / ln(hCG2 / hCG1)
  • What it measures: How long it takes for your hCG concentration to double, expressed in hours
  • Normal range: 48-72 hours when hCG is below 1,200 mIU/mL (first ~5 weeks). Slows to 72-96 hours at higher levels.
  • Important: A single hCG value is less meaningful than the trend. Serial measurements (2+ draws, 48-72 hours apart) give your doctor the clearest picture.
  • Limitations: hCG levels vary widely between normal pregnancies. This calculator estimates trends but cannot diagnose pregnancy viability.

Methodology

Doubling Time = (Hours x ln(2)) / ln(hCG2/hCG1). 48-hour rise compared against 49% minimum (Barnhart 2004).

hCG doubling: 48-72 hours below 1,200 mIU/mL. Rate slows as levels rise. Minimum viable 48-hour rise: 49%.
