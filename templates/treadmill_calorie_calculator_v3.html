Methodology

This calculator uses the ACSM Metabolic Equations:

Walking: VO2 = 0.1 x S + 1.8 x S x G + 3.5

Running: VO2 = 0.2 x S + 0.9 x S x G + 3.5

Where S = speed in m/min, G = fractional grade. Calories = VO2 x weight(kg) x duration(min) / 200. Linear interpolation is used in the 3.7-5.0 mph transition zone.

A treadmill burns roughly 200-600 calories per 30 minutes depending on body weight, speed, and incline. Using the ACSM metabolic equations: walking at 3.5 mph flat burns about 177 kcal for a 155 lb person. At 10% incline, that nearly doubles to 352 kcal. Running at 6 mph flat burns about 357 kcal. The 12-3-30 workout (12% incline, 3 mph, 30 min) burns approximately 420 kcal with low joint impact.
