Frequently Asked Questions

  • Running burns approximately 80-140 calories per mile depending on body weight. A 155-lb runner burns roughly 100 calories per mile on flat terrain.

  • Running faster burns more calories per minute but only marginally more per mile (about 10-12% difference). Focus on running farther for more total calorie burn.

  • Yes. A 5% grade increases burn by about 18%, and an 8% grade by about 30%. Trail running adds about 12% due to uneven surface.

  • A 30-minute run burns 240-400 calories for most people. A 155-lb runner at 10 min/mile pace burns roughly 295 calories.

Methodology

Uses MET values interpolated from the Ainsworth Compendium based on pace (min/mile). Formula: Calories = MET x weight(kg) x duration(hours). Terrain multipliers based on ACSM grade equations. Net calories = gross minus resting metabolic rate during exercise.

Running calorie burn depends on weight, pace, and terrain. A 155-lb runner at 10 min/mile pace burns about 100 calories per mile gross. For a 5K (3.1 miles), that is roughly 310 calories. Running uphill increases burn by 8-30% depending on grade. The MET formula is: Calories = MET x weight(kg) x hours.
