How This Calculator Works

This tool compares your child's age, gender, height, and weight against CDC and WHO growth charts. It calculates percentiles for:

  • Height: Compared with children of the same age and gender
  • Weight: Based on standard growth curves
  • BMI: Age- and gender-adjusted BMI percentile

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a growth percentile?

A percentile shows how your child compares to others. For example, the 80th percentile in height means your child is taller than 80% of peers.

Which data sources are used?

This calculator uses CDC and WHO growth charts, depending on the age range and regional selection.

Is the BMI percentile accurate?

It's a standard pediatric tool, but only your pediatrician can interpret results in a medical context.
