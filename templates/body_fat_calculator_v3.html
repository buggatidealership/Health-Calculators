Frequently Asked Questions

  • The U.S. Navy method estimates body fat percentage using circumference measurements of the neck, waist, and hips (for women), along with height. Developed by Hodgdon and Beckett in 1984 at the Naval Health Research Center, it uses logarithmic equations validated against hydrostatic weighing.

  • The Navy method typically estimates body fat within 3-4 percentage points of DEXA scan results. Accuracy decreases at extreme body fat levels. For tracking changes over time, the Navy method is highly effective when measurements are taken consistently.

  • According to the ACE: Men — Essential 2-5%, Athletes 6-13%, Fitness 14-17%, Average 18-24%, Obese 25%+. Women — Essential 10-13%, Athletes 14-20%, Fitness 21-24%, Average 25-31%, Obese 32%+. The fitness range is generally considered optimal for long-term health.

  • BMI only considers height and weight and cannot distinguish between muscle and fat. A muscular athlete may have an "overweight" BMI but very low body fat. Body fat percentage directly measures your fat-to-lean ratio, giving a more accurate picture of body composition and health risks.

  • For the Navy method, measure your waist at the navel (belly button) level. Stand relaxed, exhale normally without sucking in your stomach, and wrap the tape horizontally around your abdomen. The tape should be snug but not compressing the skin.

Methodology

This calculator uses the Hodgdon-Beckett equations (U.S. Navy method, 1984).

Men: BF% = 86.010 x log10(waist - neck) - 70.041 x log10(height) + 36.76

Women: BF% = 163.205 x log10(waist + hip - neck) - 97.684 x log10(height) + 36.76

All measurements are converted to centimeters before calculation. Standard error of estimate is approximately 3.5% for men and 3.7% for women compared to hydrostatic weighing. Body fat categories follow the ACE classification system.

Body fat percentage is the proportion of your total body weight that is fat tissue. The U.S. Navy method estimates body fat from neck, waist, and hip circumference measurements using logarithmic equations. For men, the formula uses waist minus neck circumference relative to height. For women, it adds hip circumference. According to the American Council on Exercise, healthy body fat ranges are: Men - Athletes 6-13%, Fitness 14-17%, Average 18-24%. Women - Athletes 14-20%, Fitness 21-24%, Average 25-31%. The Navy method is accurate within 3-4% of DEXA scan results for most individuals.
