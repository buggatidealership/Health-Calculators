Subway Calorie Range: What to Expect

A Subway 6-inch sandwich typically ranges from 200 to 500 calories, and a footlong from 400 to over 1,000 calories. The range is wide because bread, protein, cheese, and sauces each contribute independent calorie loads. A 6-inch Veggie Delite on Italian White with no sauce is around 200 calories; a Footlong Tuna on Italian Herbs & Cheese bread with mayo, cheese, and avocado can exceed 1,200 calories.

Understanding what drives calories at Subway is the first step to making an informed order. The four primary calorie contributors are:

  1. Sandwich size: Going from 6-inch to footlong roughly doubles the bread and protein calories.
  2. Protein choice: Turkey (60 cal) vs. Tuna with mayo (200 cal) vs. Meatball Marinara (240 cal) — a 180-calorie swing from protein alone.
  3. Sauces: Mayo adds 110 calories per serving. Ordering a sandwich with two sauces (e.g., mayo + ranch) can add over 200 calories before counting anything else.
  4. Bread type: Italian White (200 cal) vs. Wrap (300 cal) — a 100-calorie difference just from the vessel.

Cheese and vegetables are minor factors by comparison. A single slice of cheese adds 40–50 calories; the entire standard veggie load (lettuce, tomato, cucumber, peppers, onion) adds about 35 calories total.

Subway Bread Comparison

Italian White bread has the fewest calories (200 per 6-inch serving). 9-Grain Wheat and Hearty Multigrain are slightly higher at 210 calories but provide significantly more fiber (3–4g vs. 1g). Wraps are highest at 300 calories. For fiber and micronutrients, 9-Grain Wheat or Hearty Multigrain are the better choices at minimal calorie cost.

Avoid the Wrap if calorie control is a priority — its 300-calorie base adds up quickly.

Protein Comparison: Lean vs. Dense

Subway's leanest proteins are Oven Roasted Turkey (60 cal, 10g protein per 6-inch serving) and Black Forest Ham (60 cal, 9g protein). The most calorie-dense are Meatball Marinara (240 cal) and Tuna with mayo (200 cal). For protein per calorie, Rotisserie Chicken (100 cal, 17g protein) and Steak (130 cal, 19g protein) are the most efficient choices.

The Hidden Calorie Trap: Sauces

Sauces are the most overlooked calorie source at Subway. Mayonnaise adds 110 calories per serving; Ranch adds 110 calories; Chipotle Southwest adds 100 calories. Ordering two mayo-based sauces can add more calories than a second slice of cheese. The safest low-calorie choices are mustard (5 cal), red wine vinegar (0 cal), honey mustard (30 cal), and sweet onion sauce (40 cal).

3 Healthiest Subway Orders (With Exact Nutrition)

The three healthiest Subway orders for different goals are: (1) for low-calorie eating — 6-inch Turkey on 9-Grain Wheat with veggies and mustard (~275 cal, 17g protein); (2) for high-protein — 6-inch Steak on 9-Grain Wheat with veggies and sweet onion sauce (~380 cal, 27g protein); (3) for high-fiber/balanced — 6-inch Rotisserie Chicken on Hearty Multigrain with spinach, avocado, and vinegar (~410 cal, 25g protein, 8g fiber).

6-inch Oven Roasted Turkey | 9-Grain Wheat | Lettuce, Tomato, Cucumber, Green Pepper, Onion, Spinach | Yellow Mustard + Vinegar

  • Calories: ~280 kcal
  • Protein: 17g
  • Carbs: 44g
  • Fat: 4g
  • Fiber: 4g

Best for: calorie restriction, quick meals, keeping lunch under 300 calories. This order leaves plenty of calorie room for dinner and snacks.

  • Calories: ~370 kcal
  • Protein: 27g
  • Carbs: 47g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 4g

Best for: active individuals, post-workout meals, or anyone targeting 25g+ protein at lunch without excess calories. See the Protein Intake Calculator to find your daily protein target.

Footlong Rotisserie Chicken | Hearty Multigrain | Full veggie load + Avocado | Sweet Onion + Olive Oil

  • Calories: ~740 kcal
  • Protein: 42g
  • Carbs: 95g
  • Fat: 19g
  • Fiber: 13g

Best for: athletes, high-activity days, or anyone who skips breakfast. This covers roughly 35-40% of daily calorie needs for most adults and provides substantial protein and fiber.

High-Protein Subway Strategy

Subway is an underrated high-protein fast food option when ordered correctly. Key strategies:

  • Double the protein: Ask for double meat on any sub to roughly double the protein portion. A double Rotisserie Chicken 6-inch provides ~41g of protein at under 450 calories total.
  • Footlong over 6-inch: Simply ordering a footlong doubles both bread and protein, giving you 34-40g protein depending on your choice without the per-unit premium of double meat.
  • Skip cheese, add avocado: Cheese adds 40-50 cal of mostly fat. Avocado adds 60 cal but provides heart-healthy monounsaturated fat, potassium, and fiber.

The Sodium Problem at Subway

Subway sandwiches are significantly higher in sodium than their calorie count suggests. A typical 6-inch turkey sub contains 700-950 mg sodium — nearly half the FDA's recommended daily maximum of 2,300 mg. Processed meats (ham, salami, pepperoni) and pickled vegetables (pickles, jalapeños, banana peppers) are the biggest sodium sources. A footlong with processed meat and multiple pickled toppings can approach or exceed 2,000 mg sodium.

The American Heart Association recommends no more than 2,300 mg of sodium per day, with an ideal target of 1,500 mg for most adults (AHA, 2021). Subway's sodium content is driven by:

  • Processed deli meats: Ham, salami, pepperoni, and cold cuts are salt-cured, contributing 480-760 mg sodium per 6-inch protein serving.
  • Bread: Each 6-inch bread option contains 300-570 mg sodium. Italian Herbs & Cheese bread is highest at ~490 mg.
  • Pickled toppings: Pickles (~115 mg), jalapeños (~200 mg), banana peppers (~180 mg), and olives (~115 mg) all contribute meaningful sodium. Loading all four adds ~610 mg.
  • Sauces: Chipotle Southwest (210 mg), Ranch (200 mg), and Mayo (80 mg) each add sodium on top of the base.

Lower-sodium strategies: choose chicken or turkey (lean meats are less processed), pick Swiss cheese (30 mg vs. American's 200 mg), avoid pickled toppings, and use oil/vinegar instead of mayo-based sauces.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the healthiest Subway sandwich?

The healthiest Subway sandwiches are the 6-inch Oven Roasted Turkey (~280 cal, 17g protein) and 6-inch Rotisserie Chicken (~310 cal, 24g protein), both on 9-Grain Wheat with vegetables and mustard or vinegar. They combine lean protein, whole grains, and minimal saturated fat.

Is Subway good for weight loss?

Subway can support weight loss when ordered strategically. Choose 6-inch subs over footlongs, select lean proteins (turkey, chicken), minimize or skip cheese, load up on free vegetables, and use mustard or vinegar instead of mayo. A well-built 6-inch can be a satisfying 280–350 calorie meal with 15-25g protein.

How does Subway compare to Chipotle for calories?

A typical Chipotle burrito bowl ranges from 500–900 calories depending on toppings, while a Subway 6-inch sandwich ranges from 270–450 calories. Subway's lower calorie floor makes it easier to stay under 400 calories at lunch. However, both restaurants can become high-calorie meals depending on sauce and topping choices. Try the Chipotle Nutrition Calculator to compare directly.

Which Subway sandwich is highest in protein?

For a standard 6-inch, Steak provides the most protein (19g), followed by Chicken Teriyaki (18g) and Rotisserie Chicken (17g). Asking for double meat multiplies these values. A footlong Steak provides approximately 40g protein, making it one of the highest-protein fast food options at that calorie level.

Is the Subway tuna sandwich healthy?

Subway's tuna sandwich is mixed nutritionally. Tuna itself is a lean, high-protein fish, but Subway mixes it with mayonnaise, which adds 17g of fat per 6-inch serving. The result is 200 calories of mostly fat with only 9g protein for the protein portion — one of the least protein-efficient options. If you want tuna's omega-3 benefits, you'd get better nutritional value from plain tuna at home.
