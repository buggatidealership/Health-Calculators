Daily Habits That Reduce Hair Loss

Healthy routines can make a big difference in how much hair you shed. These simple changes support scalp health and reduce breakage.

  • Wash hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on roots
  • Massage your scalp for 5 minutes a day
  • Limit heat styling and harsh chemicals
  • Use a silk pillowcase to reduce friction

Medical Treatments and Supplements

If lifestyle changes aren't enough, medical support can help. Start with over-the-counter treatments or consult a dermatologist.

  • Minoxidil: FDA-approved topical for both men and women
  • Finasteride: Oral medication for male pattern baldness
  • Biotin: Supports keratin production (often used in hair supplements)
  • Iron, Zinc, Vitamin D: Deficiencies in these nutrients may trigger shedding
  • PRP or microneedling: Procedures that stimulate new growth

Frequently Asked Questions

Can stress really cause hair loss?

Yes. Stress-related shedding is called telogen effluvium. It's temporary and usually resolves in a few months.

Does cutting your hair make it grow faster?

No. Cutting hair reduces split ends, but growth happens at the scalp — not the ends.

When should I see a doctor?

If you're losing large amounts of hair suddenly or see bald patches, book an appointment with a dermatologist.
