CC to C Cup Conversion: The Essentials

The amount of cubic centimeters (cc) needed to achieve a C cup varies based on your chest measurements, but here are the general guidelines from board-certified plastic surgeons:

Band Size CCs for C Cup Notes
30 250-300cc Smaller frame requires less volume
32 300-350cc Most common band size
34 350-400cc Standard volume range
36 400-450cc Wider chest needs more volume
38 450-500cc Larger frame requires more volume

Important note: These are approximate ranges. The exact volume needed depends on your existing breast tissue, chest width, and tissue characteristics.

Why CC Measurements Aren't the Same as Cup Sizes

Many patients mistakenly believe there's a one-to-one correlation between CCs and cup sizes. Here's why that's not accurate:

  • Cup sizes are relative to band size (a 34C has more volume than a 32C)
  • The same implant volume looks different on different body frames
  • Current breast tissue affects final cup size outcome
  • Implant placement (over vs. under muscle) changes apparent size
  • Implant profile (projection) affects how volume is distributed

This is why consultation with a board-certified plastic surgeon is essential for accurate sizing.

The "C Cup Formula" for Implant Sizing

While there's no universal formula, surgeons often use these calculations as a starting point:

  • Starting with nothing (AA cup): ~300cc + (25cc × each band size over 32)
  • Starting with A cup: ~200cc + (25cc × each band size over 32)
  • Starting with B cup: ~100cc + (25cc × each band size over 32)

Example: If you're currently a 34A and want to be a C cup, you would need approximately 250cc implants (200cc base + 50cc for being 2 band sizes over 32).

Factors That Affect How Many CCs You Need

  • Chest width: Wider chest walls require more volume for the same cup size
  • Tissue elasticity: Tighter tissue may resist expansion, requiring more volume
  • Implant profile: Higher profile implants create more projection with less width
  • Placement: Submuscular (under muscle) placement can make implants appear smaller
  • Existing tissue: More natural breast tissue means less implant volume needed

FAQs About C Cup Implant Sizing

Is 300cc a C cup?

A 300cc implant typically creates a C cup on a 32 band size, a small C on a 34 band, and a B cup on a 36 band size. Implant volume must increase with band size to maintain the same cup appearance.

How do I know if a C cup will look natural on me?

The most natural results come from implants that match your body proportions. A board-certified plastic surgeon will measure your chest width and recommend an appropriate range. For most women, C cups offer a balanced increase without appearing obviously augmented.

Can implant size be precisely predicted?

No. While surgeons can estimate cup size outcomes, many factors affect the final result. This is why surgeons use sizers during your consultation to visualize approximate outcomes rather than promising specific cup sizes.

What's the difference between 300cc moderate and 300cc high profile?

With the same volume, a high-profile implant creates more projection (sticks out more) but has a narrower base, while a moderate-profile implant creates a more subtle projection but covers more chest width. The same volume can look like different cup sizes depending on profile.
