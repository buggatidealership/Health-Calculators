Frequently Asked Questions

  • 250-500 calories above TDEE for most people. A lean surplus (+250) minimizes fat gain. Aggressive (+700) maximizes muscle but adds more fat.

  • Beginners, detrained, and higher body fat individuals can. For trained lifters, at least 200-300 extra calories is needed for maximal growth.

  • 1.6-2.2 g/kg/day (0.73-1.0 g/lb). This calculator uses 1 g/lb as a practical target supported by meta-analysis.

  • 3-6 months is typical. Bulk until body fat reaches ~15-17% (men) or ~25-27% (women), then cut.

  • Reduce surplus by 100-200 calories. Verify activity level selection. Ensure you're following a structured resistance training program.

Methodology

BMR via Mifflin-St Jeor equation, multiplied by activity factor (1.2-1.9) for TDEE. Surplus options: +250 (lean), +400 (moderate), +700 (aggressive). Macros: 1 g protein/lb bodyweight, 25% calories from fat, remainder from carbs. Weekly gain = surplus x 7 / 3500.

To build muscle, eat TDEE + 250-500 calories per day. Mifflin-St Jeor equation estimates BMR, multiplied by activity factor for TDEE. Protein: 1 g per pound bodyweight. Fat: 25% of calories. Carbs: remaining calories. A 250 cal surplus gains ~0.5 lb/week. Beginners can use larger surpluses (500-700 cal). Rate of muscle gain slows with training experience.
