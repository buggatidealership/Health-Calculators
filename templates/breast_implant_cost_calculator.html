What Does Breast Augmentation Cost?

Breast augmentation costs include the surgeon's fee, facility fee, anesthesia, and implant cost. According to the American Society of Plastic Surgeons 2024 statistics, the average surgeon fee is $4,294, with total procedure costs ranging from $7,500 to $15,000 depending on implant type, geographic location, and surgeon experience.

Medical Disclaimer: This tool provides educational cost estimates only. For an exact quote, consult a board-certified plastic surgeon in your area.

How the Breast Implant Cost Calculator Works

This calculator provides cost estimates for breast augmentation surgery based on several key factors that influence pricing:

Regional Price Variations

Breast augmentation costs vary significantly by region and even within countries. This calculator accounts for:

  • Country: Base costs are different in each country due to economic factors, healthcare systems, and market competition.
  • Location Type: Major metropolitan areas typically charge more than smaller cities or rural areas within the same country.

Implant Selection Impact

Your choice of implant material and size affects total cost:

  • Implant Type: Saline implants are generally less expensive than silicone, while specialized options like gummy bear or structured implants command premium prices.
  • Size Range: Larger implant sizes may require more specialized surgical techniques or additional materials, potentially increasing costs.

Surgeon Experience Factor

The surgeon's experience level is a major pricing factor:

  • Top Specialists: Surgeons with exceptional reputations, extensive before/after portfolios, and specialized training typically charge 30-50% more than average.
  • Board-Certified: Standard pricing from qualified surgeons with appropriate certifications and moderate experience.
  • Less Experienced: Surgeons with fewer years of experience or those in training may offer lower rates to build their practice.

Important: This calculator provides estimates based on aggregated data from multiple sources and should be used for planning purposes only. Actual costs can vary, and a consultation with a board-certified plastic surgeon is necessary for an accurate quote.

Frequently Asked Questions About Breast Implant Costs

How much do breast implants cost?

In the United States, the total cost for breast augmentation typically ranges from $5,000 to $10,000. The cost breakdown generally includes:

  • Surgeon's fee: $2,500-$4,500
  • Anesthesia: $600-$1,000
  • Facility fee: $800-$1,200
  • Implants: $1,000-$3,000
  • Miscellaneous (medical tests, garments, etc.): $200-$500

International costs vary significantly, with some countries like Thailand or Mexico offering procedures at 40-60% of US prices, while others like Australia may be more expensive.

Does insurance cover breast implants?

Insurance coverage for breast implants depends on whether the procedure is considered cosmetic or reconstructive:

  • Cosmetic Augmentation: Almost never covered by health insurance
  • Reconstructive (Post-Mastectomy): Covered by most insurance plans in the US under the Women's Health and Cancer Rights Act of 1998
  • Congenital Deformities: May be covered if considered medically necessary
  • Severe Asymmetry: Sometimes covered if the asymmetry is significant enough to be considered a deformity

Always check directly with your insurance provider about coverage options and requirements.

Are silicone implants more expensive than saline?

Yes, silicone implants typically cost more than saline implants:

  • Saline implants: $1,000-$1,500 per pair
  • Standard silicone implants: $2,000-$2,500 per pair
  • Cohesive gel ("gummy bear") implants: $2,500-$3,000 per pair
  • Structured or specialized implants: $2,500-$3,500 per pair

The manufacturing process for silicone implants is more complex, and the material itself is more expensive. However, many patients feel the additional cost is justified by the more natural look and feel silicone provides.

What factors affect breast implant costs?

Several factors can influence the total cost of breast implant surgery:

  • Geographic Location: Prices vary by country and even by city within the same country
  • Surgeon Experience: More experienced surgeons with stronger reputations typically charge more
  • Implant Type: Saline, silicone, gummy bear, or structured implants have different price points
  • Implant Brand: Premium brands like Mentor, Allergan, or Sientra may cost more than others
  • Procedure Complexity: Whether you need a lift in addition to augmentation
  • Anesthesia Type: General anesthesia costs more than local anesthesia with sedation
  • Facility Type: Hospital operating rooms cost more than outpatient surgical centers
  • Pre-operative Testing: Required medical tests can add to the total cost

Always ask for an itemized quote to understand exactly what is included in your total price.

Is medical tourism for breast implants worth it?

Medical tourism for breast implants offers potential savings but comes with important considerations:

Potential Benefits:

  • Cost savings: 40-70% less expensive in countries like Thailand, Mexico, or Brazil
  • Combined vacation opportunity
  • Access to surgeons who may specialize in specific techniques

Significant Risks:

  • Difficulty researching surgeon credentials and facility standards
  • Potential language barriers affecting communication
  • Complications after returning home requiring local treatment
  • Different implant brands may not be FDA-approved
  • Limited legal recourse if problems occur
  • Travel restrictions during recovery period
  • Difficulty with follow-up appointments

If considering medical tourism, thoroughly research the surgeon's credentials, read patient reviews, verify the clinic's accreditation, and have a plan for follow-up care after returning home.
