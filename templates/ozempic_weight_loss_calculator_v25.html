Frequently Asked Questions

  • In the STEP 1 clinical trial (N=1,961), participants taking semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly lost an average of 14.9% of their body weight over 68 weeks, compared to 2.4% with placebo. At the standard Ozempic doses (0.5 mg and 1.0 mg), the SUSTAIN trials showed weight loss typically ranges from 5% to 10% of body weight over 6-12 months.

  • Most people start noticing weight loss within 4-6 weeks of starting treatment. The rate of loss is typically fastest between weeks 12 and 28 during dose escalation, then gradually slows as the body approaches a new equilibrium.

  • Yes. All STEP clinical trials combined semaglutide with a reduced-calorie diet (500 kcal/day deficit) and increased physical activity (150 minutes/week). Semaglutide reduces appetite and food cravings, making it easier to sustain a calorie deficit, but dietary changes amplify the results substantially.

  • The STEP 4 trial showed participants who switched from semaglutide 2.4 mg to placebo regained approximately two-thirds of the weight they had lost over the following 48 weeks. Maintaining diet and exercise habits after discontinuation can reduce regain.

  • Yes. The projections are derived from the STEP (Semaglutide Treatment Effect in People with obesity) and SUSTAIN clinical trial programs. STEP 1 was published in the New England Journal of Medicine (Wilding et al., 2021, N=1,961).

Methodology

This calculator projects weight loss using dose-specific percentage outcomes from published clinical trials. For the 2.4 mg dose, projections are based on the STEP 1 trial (14.9% average body weight loss at 68 weeks). For the 1.0 mg and 0.5 mg doses, projections use data from the SUSTAIN trial program (~8% and ~5% at 52 weeks respectively).

Weight loss is calculated as a percentage of starting body weight and scaled proportionally to the selected treatment duration. The calculator does not account for non-linear trajectories, dose titration timing, or presence of type 2 diabetes. This tool provides estimates only and does not replace clinical guidance.

Ozempic (semaglutide) is a GLP-1 receptor agonist. In the STEP 1 clinical trial, the 2.4 mg weekly dose produced 14.9% average body weight loss over 68 weeks. The standard Ozempic doses (0.5 mg and 1.0 mg) produce approximately 5-8% weight loss over 52 weeks in the SUSTAIN trials. Weight loss is fastest between weeks 12-28 during dose escalation. The FDA recommends no more than 400 mg of caffeine per day for healthy adults. All trials combined medication with a 500 kcal/day calorie deficit and 150 minutes/week of exercise.
