What Is Breast Implant Sizing?

Breast implant sizing uses body measurements — chest width, breast base diameter, and tissue characteristics — to determine optimal implant dimensions. This calculator applies the Tebbetts-Adams "High Five" decision process (2005), which uses five measurements taken in five minutes to guide implant selection for predictable outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right breast implant size?

Choosing the right implant size involves balancing your aesthetic goals with your body's anatomical constraints. Key factors include your chest/breast width, tissue thickness, and desired outcome. While this calculator provides a starting point based on cup size difference and measurements, the final decision should be made in consultation with a board-certified plastic surgeon who can assess your unique anatomy and recommend appropriate options.

What's the difference between implant types?

The three main types of implants differ in material composition, feel, and cost. Silicone implants contain a cohesive gel that closely mimics natural breast tissue. Saline implants contain sterile salt water and typically cost less, but may feel less natural. "Gummy bear" or highly cohesive gel implants maintain their shape even if the outer shell is broken and typically offer the most natural appearance with the highest price point.

How long do breast implants last?

Breast implants are not considered lifetime devices. Most manufacturers suggest replacement every 10-20 years, though many women have implants much longer without complications. The need for replacement is typically based on changes in the implant (rupture, hardening) or changes in your body/preferences rather than a strict timeline. Regular monitoring through mammograms, MRIs, or ultrasounds is recommended.

What are the risks of breast implant surgery?

Common risks include capsular contracture (hardening of scar tissue around implants), rupture/deflation, changes in nipple/breast sensation, asymmetry, and cosmetic dissatisfaction. Rarer complications include BIA-ALCL (a type of lymphoma associated with textured implants), infection, and implant displacement. Long-term maintenance including possible revision surgeries should be considered when planning for implants.

Do breast implants cause weight gain?

Breast implants themselves add only 1-3 pounds of weight (0.5-1.5 pounds each). Any additional weight gain after surgery is typically due to temporary factors like post-surgical fluid retention, reduced physical activity during recovery, and medication effects. Scientific studies show no evidence that implants affect metabolism or cause systemic weight gain. For an in-depth analysis of how implants affect body weight, see our comprehensive guide on breast implants and weight gain.

Does insurance cover breast implants?

Health insurance typically doesn't cover cosmetic breast augmentation or its complications. However, reconstructive procedures (after mastectomy or for severe asymmetry) may be covered. Some surgeons offer financing options or payment plans. The total cost includes not just the implants but surgeon's fees, anesthesia, facility costs, and follow-up care, which this calculator attempts to estimate.

Do breast implants affect mammogram screening?

Yes, breast implants can affect mammogram imaging, requiring specialized techniques and additional views to properly screen breast tissue. While the risk of implant rupture during a mammogram is very low (less than 1%), you should always inform your imaging facility about your implants beforehand.
